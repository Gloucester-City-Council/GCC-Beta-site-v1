Passenger information

Understand your rights and what to expect when using a licensed hackney carriage or private hire vehicle in Gloucester.

Your rights as a passenger

Assistance dogs

Licensed drivers must carry your guide dog, hearing dog or other assistance dog. There is no extra charge. A driver can only refuse if they hold a medical exemption certificate issued by Gloucester City Council.

Smokefree vehicles

All licensed vehicles are smokefree at all times. This includes cigarettes, e-cigarettes and vaping. 'No Smoking' signs must be displayed in all vehicles.

Seatbelts

All passengers must wear a seatbelt. Drivers are responsible for ensuring passengers under 14 years old are wearing a seatbelt.

Fares and payment

Hackney carriages (taxis)

For journeys within the Gloucester City district, the fare must not exceed the amount shown on the meter. The taximeter and a tariff card showing the approved fares must be clearly visible to you.

Private hire and out-of-district journeys

For private hire vehicles, or hackney carriage journeys outside the district, the fare or the way it will be calculated must be agreed with you before the journey starts.

Card payments

All licensed vehicles must accept contactless and card payments. You can ask for a receipt at any time.

You should always agree the fare before you travel on a private hire journey. Ask the driver for the fare or how it will be calculated at the time of booking.

Accessibility for wheelchair users

All newly licensed hackney carriages must be wheelchair accessible. Accessible vehicles must meet the following minimum standards:

  • Ramps or a lift, with locking and stowage mechanisms
  • Anchorage points for both the wheelchair and the occupant
  • Grab handles for passengers
  • Doorway width of at least 75cm
  • Doorway height of at least 1.2m
  • Compartment height of at least 1.3m
  • Non-slip treads on all steps

How to make a complaint

All licensed vehicles must display a notice telling you how to make a complaint. The notice will include the council's contact details, the vehicle's plate number and the council's website address.

Complaints are recorded and investigated. Serious complaints may be referred to the police.

When making a complaint

It helps if you can provide the vehicle's plate number (shown on the rear of the vehicle and inside the car), the date and time of the journey, and a description of what happened.

To make a complaint, contact the licensing team:

  • Email: licensing@gloucester.gov.uk
  • Phone: 01452 396396

Lost property

Drivers must check their vehicle at the end of each journey for items left behind.

If an item is not collected within 48 hours, the driver must hand it to the council or the police, and get a receipt.

If you have lost something in a licensed vehicle, contact us:

  • Email: licensing@gloucester.gov.uk
  • Phone: 01452 396396

Safety and vehicle checks

All licensed vehicles are regularly inspected by the council:

  • Annual inspection for all licensed vehicles
  • Every 6 months for vehicles aged 8 years or more

All vehicles must carry a BSEN3 1kg powder fire extinguisher (in date) and a first aid kit meeting HSE standards.

Some vehicles may have CCTV or dashcams installed. Where fitted, these must have been approved by the council and must comply with data protection law.
