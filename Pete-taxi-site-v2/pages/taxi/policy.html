Legislation

Hackney carriage and private hire licensing in Gloucester is governed by the following legislation:

  • Town Police Clauses Act 1847
  • Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1976 (Part II)
  • Equality Act 2010 – covers assistance dogs and non-discrimination
  • Licensing Act 2003 – covers restrictions on alcohol sales
  • Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 (PACE) – covers interviews under caution
  • Department for Transport Statutory Taxi and Private Hire Vehicle Standards (July 2020)

Aims of the service

The council's licensing service aims to:

  • Protect public health and safety
  • Establish a professional, respected trade
  • Ensure access to efficient, effective public transport
  • Protect the environment
  • Improve standards and visibility in support of city regeneration

Corporate Plan 2025 to 2028

The council's Corporate Plan sets out the following commitments for hackney carriage and private hire licensing:

  • Work with operators to increase the number of zero-emission hackney carriage and private hire vehicles
  • Retain the Purple Flag for the night-time economy
  • Develop safer spaces, especially for women and girls

Quality and standards

The council maintains high standards through:

  • Enhanced DBS checks for drivers every 6 months (via the DBS Update Service)
  • Annual vehicle inspections (every 6 months for vehicles aged 8 years or more)
  • Mandatory booking records with defined fields and a minimum 6-month retention period
  • Driver self-reporting of relevant incidents within 48 hours
  • Notification of vehicle accidents within 72 hours
  • Renewal applications submitted before the current licence expires

Consistency across Gloucestershire

Gloucester City Council works with other Gloucestershire licensing authorities to maintain consistent standards:

  • Adopted common standards across Gloucestershire authorities
  • Joint authorisation of enforcement officers
  • Information-sharing agreements between authorities
  • Use of the National Register of Taxi Licence Refusals and Revocations (NR3) to check refusals and revocations nationally

Enforcement and accountability

How decisions are made

Licensing decisions are made by:

  • Licensing Officers – for routine decisions within agreed delegations
  • Licensing and Enforcement Sub-Committee – for decisions on refusals, suspensions and revocations
  • Full Licensing Committee – for policy decisions

Penalty points system

The council operates a totting-up penalty points system for drivers. Accumulating more than 12 points in any 12-month period triggers a Sub-Committee review.

Immediate action

The council can take immediate action to suspend or revoke a licence where public safety is at risk. This power is available for both driver and vehicle licences.

Right of appeal

Anyone whose licence has been refused, suspended or revoked has 21 days to appeal to the Magistrates' Court. In some cases, an appeal may be made to the Crown Court instead.

Note that the right of appeal does not apply to immediate vehicle suspensions under section 68 of the Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1976.
