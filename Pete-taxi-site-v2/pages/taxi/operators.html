Operator licensing

Apply for a private hire operator licence in Gloucester. Find out about your responsibilities, record keeping, staff checks and fees.

Who needs an operator licence

You need a private hire operator licence if you accept bookings for private hire vehicles in Gloucester, whether as a business or a sole trader. Hackney carriages do not require an operator licence.

Your operator base must be within the Gloucester City boundaries. A licence usually lasts 5 years, though the council may grant a shorter term.

Fit and proper person

All directors, partners and operators must pass a fit and proper person assessment. This applies to both new applicants and renewals.

DBS checks and staff

Operator DBS check

If you are not also a licensed driver with Gloucester City Council, you must complete an annual Basic Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check.

Booking and dispatch staff

All staff who take bookings or dispatch vehicles must have a Basic DBS check. You must set a policy for how often these are renewed. The common standard is every 3 years.

Employing people with criminal records

You must have a written policy on employing people with criminal records. The policy must also state when re-checks are required.

Outsourced booking and dispatch

If you use a third-party provider for booking or dispatch, you must ensure they meet the same DBS and policy requirements as your own staff.

Record keeping

You must keep a record of every booking. Each record must include:

  • Passenger name
  • Time the booking was made
  • Pick-up point
  • Destination
  • Driver name
  • Driver licence number
  • Vehicle registration number
  • Name of the person who took the booking
  • Name of the person who dispatched the vehicle

How long to keep records

You must keep booking records for at least 6 months from the date of the last entry. Records must be made available to council officers or the police on request.

Vehicle and driver records

You must also keep details of the vehicles you operate (owner, registration and plate number) and the drivers you use (name and badge number).

Charging and booking rules

  • For journeys outside the Gloucester City district, you must agree the fare or method of calculating it before the journey starts
  • You must not use the words 'taxi' or 'cab' in any advertising or on any vehicle
  • You may only use drivers and vehicles licensed by Gloucester City Council
  • You may subcontract bookings to other licensed private hire operators under the Deregulation Act 2015

Fees for 2025 to 2026

Fees are based on the size of your fleet.

Enforcement and appeals

All licensed vehicles must display information about how to make a complaint, including the council's contact details, the plate number and the website address.

Depending on the severity and history of any concerns, the council can issue warnings, suspend your licence or revoke it. Serious cases may be referred for prosecution.

Right of appeal

If the council refuses, suspends or revokes your licence, you have 21 days to appeal to the Magistrates' Court. Note that the right of appeal does not apply to immediate vehicle suspensions under section 68 of the Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1976.
