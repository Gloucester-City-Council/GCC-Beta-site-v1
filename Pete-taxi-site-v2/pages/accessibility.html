Accessibility statement

This statement applies to the hackney carriage and private hire licensing pages on the Gloucester City Council website.

Our accessibility commitment

Gloucester City Council is committed to making its websites accessible, in accordance with the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018.

This website aims to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 AA standard as a minimum. We review our accessibility criteria regularly to make sure we continue to meet our standards.

Accessibility features

This service has been designed with accessibility in mind. It includes:

  • A skip link to bypass the header and go directly to the main content
  • A high contrast mode toggle in the header of every page
  • Clear focus states that meet WCAG 2.2 contrast requirements
  • Minimum touch target sizes of 44px for all interactive elements
  • Keyboard navigation support throughout, including accessible tab interfaces
  • ARIA labels and landmark roles for screen reader users
  • Descriptive link text that explains the action or destination
  • Sentence case headings in plain English with a maximum reading age of 11
  • Colour is never used as the only way to convey meaning
  • Reduced motion support for users who prefer less animation
  • Works across a range of browsers, devices and assistive technologies

Known accessibility issues

We are not aware of any significant accessibility issues at this time. If you find a problem, please let us know using the contact details below.

Tell us about an accessibility problem

If you cannot access any part of this service, or if you find an accessibility problem we have not listed here, please contact us:

  • Email: licensing@gloucester.gov.uk
  • Phone: 01452 396396

We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 5 working days.

Enforcement procedure

The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations 2018. If you are not satisfied with our response, contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS).

Preparation of this statement

This statement was prepared in March 2026. It was last reviewed in March 2026.

This website was tested against WCAG 2.2 AA using both automated tools and manual review, including keyboard navigation testing.
