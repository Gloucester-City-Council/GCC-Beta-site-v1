Driver licensing

Apply for or renew a hackney carriage or private hire driver licence in Gloucester. This page covers eligibility, required checks, training, fees and your conduct obligations.

Eligibility requirements

To apply for a hackney carriage or private hire driver licence you must meet all of the following.

  • Aged 21 or over
  • Held a full UK driving licence for at least 12 months
  • No more than 3 current DVLA penalty points (new applicants)
  • Pass the fit and proper person assessment

Licence duration

Licences are normally granted for 3 years. The council may grant a shorter term at its discretion.

Foreign nationals and applicants who have lived abroad

You must provide addresses for the last 5 years. A certificate of good conduct is required for every country you have lived in for more than 6 months since the age of 18. Certified translations are needed if not in English. Applications from those on a student visa are not accepted.

Checks and clearances

DBS check
Enhanced DBS (Other Workforce) with Child and Adult Barred Lists. Rechecked every 6 months — you must subscribe to the DBS Update Service.
NR3 check
All new and renewal applications are checked against the National Register of Taxi Licence Refusals and Revocations. Refusals and revocations are recorded on the register.
Right to work
You must demonstrate your right to work in the United Kingdom.
Medical (Group 2 standard)
Initial certificate must be no more than 3 months old. From age 45: every 5 years. From age 65: annually.

Assessments and training

You must pass a driving assessment with an approved provider. This must be retaken every 10 years.

Hackney carriage: Tests knowledge of local regulations and topographical knowledge of the area.

Private hire: Tests basic literacy and numeracy.

Safeguarding training is mandatory for all applicants. A refresher course is required every 3 years.

You must pass an English proficiency test unless you can provide an appropriate qualification certificate demonstrating English language ability.

Driver conduct and obligations

Licensed drivers must meet the following standards at all times.

  • Wear your badge so it is clearly visible to passengers
  • Be polite and punctual
  • Assist passengers with luggage and give reasonable assistance to disabled passengers
  • Take the shortest or most economical route unless the passenger asks otherwise
  • Keep all licensed vehicles smokefree — this includes e-cigarettes and vaping
  • Keep the volume of audio low; turn off the radio if asked by a passenger
  • Carry guide dogs and assistance dogs at no extra charge unless you have a medical exemption
  • Provide a receipt if asked
  • Use hands-free only when using a mobile phone while driving
  • Maintain a clean and tidy appearance

Self-reporting duty

You must notify the licensing team within 48 hours if any of the following happen:

  • Arrest, release, charge or conviction for a sexual offence, dishonesty or violence
  • Any conviction, caution, fixed penalty or pending court case
  • Any motoring offence

You must also inform the police of your licensed driver status if you are arrested or interviewed under caution.

Enforcement and appeals

A penalty points system applies. If you accumulate more than 12 penalty points in any 12-month period, your case will be referred to the Licensing and Enforcement Sub-Committee for review.

In cases where there is a risk to public safety, the council may immediately suspend or revoke your licence without prior notice.

Appealing a decision

You have 21 days to appeal against a licensing decision to the Magistrates' Court, or to the Crown Court where applicable.

Ready to apply?

Before you apply, make sure you have read all the requirements above and have the necessary documents ready. Applications are made by contacting the licensing team.
