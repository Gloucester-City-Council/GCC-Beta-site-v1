Licensing policy

The policy framework, legislation and regulatory guidelines that govern hackney carriage and private hire licensing at Gloucester City Council.

Legislation

The licensing of hackney carriage and private hire vehicles in Gloucester is governed by the following legislation.

  • Town Police Clauses Act 1847
  • Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1976 (Part II)
  • Equality Act 2010 — assistance dogs and non-discrimination
  • Licensing Act 2003 — restrictions on the sale of alcohol in licensed vehicles
  • Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 (PACE) — interviews under caution
  • Department for Transport (DfT) Statutory Taxi and Private Hire Vehicle Standards (July 2020)

Corporate strategy

The licensing service supports the following commitments in Gloucester City Council's Corporate Plan 2025 to 2028.

  • Work with operators to increase the number of zero-emission hackney carriage and private hire vehicles in Gloucester
  • Retain Purple Flag status in the night-time economy
  • Deliver safer spaces work, particularly for women and girls

Policy and guidance documents

Approved rule books and guidelines

  • Hackney Carriage Rule Book — approved June 2025
  • Private Hire Rule Book — approved June 2025
  • Hackney Carriage and Private Hire Regulatory Guidelines — approved September 2021
  • Common Standards for Licensing Hackney Carriage and Private Hire in Gloucestershire — based on DfT standards

Operational guidance

  • Enforcement and complaints policy and procedure; penalty points scheme; immediate suspension and revocation process
  • Approved testing stations list (2024) and Licensing Authority Vehicle Test Certificate requirements
  • National Register of Taxi Licence Refusals and Revocations (NR3) — use for checks and recording
  • Record-keeping requirements for operators — mandatory booking fields; minimum 6-month retention

Regulatory objectives

The licensing service exists to achieve the following objectives.

  • Protect public health and safety
  • Establish a professional, respected taxi and private hire trade
  • Ensure access to efficient and effective public transport
  • Protect the environment
  • Improve standards and visibility in support of city regeneration

Quality standards

Driver DBS checks
Six-monthly Enhanced DBS checks for all licensed drivers via the DBS Update Service subscription.
Vehicle inspections
Annual inspections for all licensed vehicles; every 6 months for vehicles aged 8 years or more.
Operator booking records
Mandatory fields required for all bookings; minimum retention period of 6 months.
Driver self-reporting
Drivers must notify the council within 48 hours of arrest, charge, conviction, caution or motoring offence.
Accident notification
Accidents involving a licensed vehicle must be reported to the council within 72 hours.

Consistency and joint working

  • Gloucester City Council has adopted common standards alongside other Gloucestershire licensing authorities
  • Enforcement officers are jointly authorised across authorities, and information-sharing agreements are in place
  • The National Register of Taxi Licence Refusals and Revocations (NR3) is used to check applicants' history nationally

Accountability and delegation

Licensing decisions are made under a Table of Delegations which sets out the responsibilities of:

  • Licensing Officers — day-to-day decisions on applications and renewals
  • Licensing and Enforcement Sub-Committee — cases referred for review, including penalty point hearings
  • Full Committee — policy decisions and appeals

Right of appeal

Any person aggrieved by a licensing decision has the right to appeal to the Magistrates' Court, or to the Crown Court where applicable, within 21 days of the decision.
