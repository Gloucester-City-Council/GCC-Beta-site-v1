Passenger information

Your rights when using licensed taxis and private hire vehicles in Gloucester. Information on fares, safety, accessibility, complaints and lost property.

Your rights and safety

Assistance dogs

All licensed drivers must carry guide dogs and assistance dogs at no extra charge. A driver may only refuse to carry an assistance dog if they have a medical exemption granted by the council.

Smokefree vehicles

All licensed hackney carriage and private hire vehicles are smokefree at all times. This includes e-cigarettes and vaping. All vehicles must display a 'No Smoking' sign. If you experience someone smoking in a licensed vehicle, you can make a complaint to the council.

Seatbelts

All passengers must wear a seatbelt. The driver is responsible for ensuring passengers under the age of 14 are wearing a seatbelt.

Driver conduct

Your driver should be polite and punctual, assist you with luggage and help you in and out of the vehicle if needed. They must take the shortest or most economical route unless you ask otherwise.

Fares and payment

Hackney carriage (taxi) fares

For journeys within the Gloucester City Council area, the driver must not charge more than the meter shows. The meter and the tariff card must be clearly visible to you throughout the journey.

Private hire and out-of-district journeys

For private hire vehicles and journeys outside the Gloucester area, the fare — or the method of calculating the fare — must be agreed with you before the journey begins.

Paying by card

All licensed vehicles must accept contactless and card payments. You can ask for a receipt after your journey.

ℹ️
If you believe you have been overcharged, you can make a complaint to the licensing team.

Wheelchair and mobility access

All newly licensed hackney carriage vehicles in Gloucester are wheelchair accessible. Wheelchair accessible vehicles (WAVs) meet the following minimum standards.

  • Ramps that can be locked and stowed securely
  • Anchorage points for the wheelchair and for the wheelchair occupant
  • Grab handles to assist passengers
  • Doorway width of at least 75 cm
  • Doorway height of at least 1.2 metres
  • Internal compartment height of at least 1.3 metres
  • Non-slip treads on steps and ramps

Vehicle safety standards

All licensed vehicles must pass regular safety inspections and carry the following equipment.

  • Annual vehicle inspection (every 6 months for vehicles that are 8 years old or more)
  • A BSEN3 1kg dry powder fire extinguisher (in date)
  • An HSE-compliant first aid kit

Some vehicles may be fitted with approved CCTV or dashcam systems, which must comply with data protection law.

Complaints and lost property

Making a complaint

All licensed vehicles must display information telling you how to make a complaint, including the council's contact details, the vehicle's plate number and the website address.

Complaints are recorded and investigated by the licensing team. Where a complaint is serious, it may be referred to the police.

Lost property

Drivers must check their vehicle for lost property after every journey. If you have left something in a licensed vehicle, contact the licensing team or the police. Items not claimed within 48 hours must be handed to the council or the police with a receipt.

Make a complaint or report lost property

Contact the licensing team by email or phone. Please have the vehicle's plate number ready if possible — this is displayed on the vehicle and is shown on your receipt.
