Operator licensing

Private hire operator licensing for companies and sole traders in Gloucester. This page covers licensing requirements, DBS checks for staff, record keeping obligations and fees.

Licensing requirements

Licence duration
Operator licences are normally granted for 5 years. The council may grant a shorter term at its discretion.
Fit and proper
The fit and proper person assessment applies to all directors and partners of the operator business.
Operator base
Your principal place of business must be within the Gloucester City Council boundary.
Vehicles and drivers
You may only dispatch Gloucester City Council licensed drivers and vehicles. You must keep records of all drivers (name, badge number) and vehicles (owner, registration and plate number) you operate.
Advertising restrictions
You must not use the words 'taxi' or 'cab' in any advertising or communications.
Sub-contracting
Sub-contracting bookings to other licensed operators is permitted under the Deregulation Act 2015.

DBS checks and staff requirements

Operator DBS
An annual basic DBS check is required for the operator (unless they are also a licensed driver with Gloucester City Council).
Booking and dispatch staff
All booking and dispatch staff must have a basic DBS check. The common standard is a recheck every 3 years. You must define your own policies on frequency and timing.
Ex-offenders policy
You must have a written policy on employing ex-offenders and on the timing of re-checks.
Outsourced booking and dispatch
If you use a third-party provider for booking or dispatch, you must ensure that provider meets the same DBS and policy requirements.

Record keeping

You must record the following information for every booking you accept or dispatch.

  • Passenger name
  • Time of the request
  • Pick-up point
  • Destination
  • Driver name
  • Driver licence number
  • Vehicle registration number
  • Name of the person who took the booking
  • Name of the person who dispatched the vehicle

Retention period: All booking records must be kept for at least 6 months from the date of the last entry.

Access: Records must be made available to council officers or police on request.

Fares and bookings

  • For journeys outside the Gloucester City Council district, a fare — or the method of calculating the fare — must be agreed with the passenger before the journey begins
  • You must not use the words 'taxi' or 'cab' in any advertising material
  • You may only dispatch Gloucester City Council licensed drivers and vehicles
  • Sub-contracting to other licensed operators is permitted under the Deregulation Act 2015

Operator licence fees 2025 to 2026

Fees are based on the size of your fleet. All fees are non-refundable.

Enforcement and appeals

  • All licensed vehicles must display information on how to make a complaint, including the council's contact details, the plate number and the website address
  • Depending on the seriousness or history of non-compliance, sanctions may include warnings, suspension, revocation or prosecution
  • You have 21 days to appeal against a licensing decision to the Magistrates' Court (except for immediate vehicle suspension under section 68 of the Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1976)
