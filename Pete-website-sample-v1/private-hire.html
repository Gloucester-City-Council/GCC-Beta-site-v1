Private hire licensing

A private hire vehicle can only be booked in advance through a licensed operator. Private hire vehicles cannot ply for hire or wait at taxi ranks.

Important difference

Private hire vehicles cannot be hailed in the street or wait at taxi ranks. They must be pre-booked through a licensed operator. Operating without proper licensing is a criminal offence.

Types of private hire licences

Driver licence

You need a private hire driver licence to drive a licensed private hire vehicle. The licence is valid for 3 years.

Vehicle licence

Your vehicle must be licensed as a private hire vehicle. Vehicle licences are valid for 1 year and must be renewed annually.

Operator licence

You must have a private hire operator licence to take bookings and dispatch private hire vehicles. This is mandatory even if you are a single driver. The licence is valid for 5 years.

Note: All three licences are required to operate legally. You cannot work as a private hire driver without being affiliated to a licensed operator.

Requirements for a driver licence

To apply for a private hire driver licence, you must:

  • be at least 18 years old
  • have held a full United Kingdom (UK) or European Economic Area (EEA) driving licence for at least 12 months
  • have the right to work in the UK
  • pass an enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check
  • pass a medical examination to Group 2 (HGV) standard
  • complete a safeguarding and child sexual exploitation course
  • pass a driving assessment
  • provide proof of address and National Insurance number
  • complete disability equality training

Requirements for a vehicle licence

Your vehicle must:

  • be less than 6 years old when first licensed
  • not be more than 10 years old (exceptional condition exemptions may apply)
  • pass a council vehicle inspection
  • have a valid MOT certificate
  • be insured for hire and reward (public hire insurance)
  • be right-hand drive
  • have at least 4 passenger seats
  • meet emission standards (ULEZ compliant)
  • have sufficient luggage capacity
  • not display taxi signage or roof lights

Executive and prestige vehicles

We license executive and prestige vehicles for private hire use. These vehicles may be subject to different age restrictions but must meet all other requirements.

Requirements for an operator licence

To apply for a private hire operator licence, you must:

  • be a fit and proper person
  • have public liability insurance
  • have suitable business premises within the district
  • keep proper booking records
  • maintain a register of all drivers and vehicles
  • have a complaints procedure
  • pass a basic DBS check (or enhanced if you also hold a driver licence)

How to apply

New driver licence

  1. Complete the online application form
  2. Pay the application fee
  3. Book and attend a medical examination (Group 2 standard)
  4. Apply for an enhanced DBS check through the council
  5. Book and pass the driving assessment
  6. Complete the safeguarding and disability equality training
  7. Provide all required documentation
  8. Attend an appointment to finalise your application

Vehicle licence

  1. Ensure your vehicle meets all requirements
  2. Book a vehicle inspection at our testing station
  3. Complete the application form with vehicle details
  4. Submit proof of insurance (hire and reward) and MOT
  5. Provide V5C registration document
  6. Pay the licence fee
  7. Collect your vehicle licence plate and internal notice

Operator licence

  1. Complete the operator application form
  2. Provide details of your business premises
  3. Submit proof of public liability insurance
  4. Provide details of booking and record-keeping systems
  5. Apply for a basic DBS check
  6. Pay the licence fee
  7. Attend an interview if required

Renewals

You should apply to renew your licence at least 6 weeks before it expires. If your licence expires, you cannot work and will need to apply as a new applicant.

Driver licence renewals require:

  • a new enhanced DBS check
  • an updated medical certificate (every 3 years, or annually if over 65)
  • proof of continued safeguarding training

Vehicle licence renewals require:

  • a fresh vehicle inspection
  • current MOT certificate
  • proof of hire and reward insurance

Conditions and obligations

As a licensed private hire driver and operator, you must:

  • only accept bookings through a licensed operator
  • display your licence plate on the rear of the vehicle
  • display the internal notice inside the vehicle
  • wear your driver badge visibly at all times when working
  • not display any taxi signage, roof lights or livery
  • not ply for hire or wait at taxi ranks
  • maintain accurate records of all bookings (operators)
  • report any convictions within 7 days
  • keep the vehicle clean and in good condition
  • comply with the council's code of conduct
  • use a properly calibrated meter if charging by time and distance

Working with app-based platforms

If you work with app-based platforms (such as Uber, Bolt, or others), you still need:

  • a private hire driver licence from this council
  • a vehicle licensed by this council
  • to work through an operator licensed by this council

The app-based company must hold a valid private hire operator licence. Check with the platform to confirm their licensing status in this district.

Enforcement and penalties

Failure to comply with licence conditions may result in:

  • a warning letter
  • penalty points on your licence
  • suspension of your licence
  • revocation of your licence
  • prosecution and unlimited fines

Operating without a valid licence is a criminal offence under the Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1976.
