Hackney carriage licensing

A hackney carriage is a vehicle licensed to ply for hire. This means it can wait at ranks or be hailed in the street by members of the public.

Important: You need separate licences for the driver, vehicle and operator. All licences must be valid to operate legally.

Types of hackney carriage licences

Driver licence

You need a hackney carriage driver licence to drive a licensed hackney carriage. The licence is valid for 3 years.

Vehicle licence

Your vehicle must be licensed as a hackney carriage. Vehicle licences are valid for 1 year and must be renewed annually.

Operator licence

If you accept bookings, you need a hackney carriage operator licence. This is valid for 5 years.

Requirements for a driver licence

To apply for a hackney carriage driver licence, you must:

  • be at least 18 years old
  • have held a full United Kingdom (UK) or European Economic Area (EEA) driving licence for at least 12 months
  • have the right to work in the UK
  • pass a Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check
  • pass a medical examination
  • pass a knowledge test of the local area
  • complete a safeguarding course
  • provide proof of address

Requirements for a vehicle licence

Your vehicle must:

  • be less than 5 years old when first licensed (or meet exceptional condition standards)
  • pass a council vehicle inspection
  • have a valid MOT certificate
  • be insured for hire and reward
  • meet accessibility requirements where applicable
  • be right-hand drive
  • have at least 4 doors
  • meet emission standards

Wheelchair accessible vehicles

We encourage the licensing of wheelchair accessible vehicles. These may be eligible for reduced licence fees and can access dedicated ranks.

How to apply

New driver licence

  1. Complete the online application form
  2. Pay the application fee
  3. Book and attend a medical examination
  4. Apply for a DBS check through the council
  5. Book and pass the knowledge test
  6. Complete the safeguarding training
  7. Attend an appointment to submit your documents

Vehicle licence

  1. Ensure your vehicle meets all requirements
  2. Book a vehicle inspection at our testing station
  3. Complete the application form with vehicle details
  4. Submit proof of insurance and MOT
  5. Pay the licence fee
  6. Collect your vehicle licence plate

Renewals

You should apply to renew your licence at least 4 weeks before it expires. If your licence expires, you will need to apply as a new applicant.

For driver licence renewals, you will need:

  • a new DBS check
  • an updated medical certificate (if your previous one has expired)
  • proof of continued safeguarding training

Conditions and obligations

As a licensed hackney carriage driver and operator, you must:

  • display your licence and fare card clearly in the vehicle
  • wear your driver badge at all times when working
  • maintain your vehicle in good condition
  • keep accurate records of all journeys
  • charge the correct metered fare
  • not refuse to carry passengers without reasonable excuse
  • not drive under the influence of alcohol or drugs
  • report any convictions, cautions or fixed penalties to the council within 7 days
  • comply with the council's code of conduct

Enforcement and penalties

Failure to comply with licence conditions may result in:

  • a warning letter
  • penalty points on your licence
  • suspension of your licence
  • revocation of your licence
  • prosecution
