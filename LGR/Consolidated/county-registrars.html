Births, marriages, deaths & civil partnerships

Registering a birth or death, ordering certificates, and booking a marriage, civil partnership or citizenship ceremony.

This is a county-wide service, run once for the whole of Gloucestershire (previously by Gloucestershire County Council). It works the same wherever you live in the county.

Newstershire Council runs the county's statutory registration service — births, deaths, marriages, civil partnerships, citizenship ceremonies, and the historical indexes. Everything is by pre-booked appointment; there's no walk-in registration.

Registering a birth

Births can only be registered in the district where they occurred, though information can be given at any register office in England or Wales and forwarded to the correct district. The baby doesn't need to attend — the hospital or health authority notifies the registrar directly.

If the parents aren't married or in a civil partnership and want both parents' details on the certificate, either parent can register alone with a signed Statutory Declaration of Acknowledgment of Parentage from the other, or the mother can register without the father's details and add them later by re-registration. A full birth certificate costs £12.50, pre-payable online or paid by card/cash at the appointment; certificates are posted, not issued on the day. To book: 01452 425060, option 2.

Registering a death

Legal deadline: within 5 days of the date of death (including weekends and bank holidays), in the district where the death occurred. If the death has been referred to the coroner, registration waits until the investigation concludes.

A death can't be registered without a Medical Certificate of Cause of Death (MCCD), sent electronically from the doctor to the register office after review by a Medical Examiner. Don't book until you've been told the MCCD has been sent — next of kin can book 24 hours after it's sent. The appointment (a private room, usually under 40 minutes) records names, addresses, relationships, dates, the deceased's occupation and any government pension. At the end you're given a reference for the free Tell Us Once service, which notifies government and council departments in one go. Death certificates cost £12.50 each. To book: 01452 425060, option 1.

Ordering certificates

Full certificates cost £12.50. A short birth certificate (no parent details) is cheaper but isn't accepted by passport offices and many organisations — check it's fit for purpose first. A £38.50 priority service issues the next working day (not weekends/bank holidays). Certificates can be collected from the Gloucestershire Heritage Hub in Gloucester, or are posted automatically after 10 days.

Marriages, civil partnerships and citizenship ceremonies

Handled through a separate site, gloucestershireregistrationservice.co.uk — ceremony booking, approved-premises licensing, and registering a building for worship and marriage. Citizenship ceremonies are booked the same way. Book via 01452 425060, option 3.

Correcting errors and re-registration

Applications to re-register a birth (for example after the parents' marriage, or to add a father's details) use specific government forms, submitted by post to Cheltenham Register Office.

Contact and offices

Phone 01452 425060 — option 1 (deaths), 2 (births), 3 (marriages/ceremonies), 4 (other). Post: Gloucestershire Registration Service, St Georges Road, Cheltenham, GL50 3EW. Eight offices operate across the county: Cheltenham, Gloucester (inside Shire Hall, appointment-only), Quedgeley, Cinderford, Stroud, Cirencester, Tewkesbury and Moreton-in-Marsh. Arriving 10+ minutes late for an appointment means rebooking.

Baby loss certificates (for pregnancy loss before 24 weeks) are a separate national scheme applied for via GOV.UK directly — the registration service has no role in issuing them.
