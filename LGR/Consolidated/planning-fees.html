Planning fees and charges

The core application fee is national; charges for invalid applications are local. Select your area to see local charges.

← All planning topics

There are two genuinely different kinds of fee here, and it's worth being precise about which is which — one is national, one isn't.

The core planning application fee

What you pay to have your application considered at all is set by national government regulation. The amount depends on the type and scale of development, and it's the same fee for the same type of application in every council in England — not something any of the Gloucestershire councils can change. Use the Planning Portal fee calculator or your council's own calculator to work out the amount for your proposal.

Administration charges for invalid applications

These are a different thing entirely — a locally-determined charge for the cost of handling an application returned because it's incomplete or doesn't meet validation requirements. They genuinely differ between councils, both in amount and in how they're triggered. See each council's figures below.

Section 106 and Biodiversity Net Gain fees

Also generally locally set (monitoring fees, registration charges), even where the underlying legal frameworks — Section 106 planning obligations, the national BNG requirement — are national.

Select your area above to see the local planning charges that apply to you.

Gloucester City Council

Gloucester's invalid-application administration charge and any local BNG/S106 monitoring fee schedule are being confirmed — not captured separately in the current source set.

Stroud District Council

£85 administration charge on planning applications submitted by email or hard copy. Applications that remain invalid after 21 days incur an additional £85 charge.

Planning fees, pre-application advice fees and BNG monitoring fees all increased from 1 April 2026 — check the current fee calculator rather than relying on a fixed figure.

Cotswold District Council

£85 administration fee on all applications submitted by email or hard copy (rather than via the Planning Portal). A separate £30 fee applies to Regulation 77 applications (habitat/species mitigation near protected sites), set nationally by government rather than the council.

Forest of Dean District Council

£30 administration charge for applications not submitted through the Planning Portal. Separate charges for handling invalid applications (from 1 April 2025): £55 for householder, advertisement, certificate of lawfulness and prior notification; £110 for minor applications; £220 for major applications — the steepest major-application charge of the councils captured.

Section 106 registration charge: £540. S106 monitoring scales with dwelling numbers: £540 (under 10 units), £1,080 (10–100), £5,380 (101–250), £10,760 (251+). BNG fees scale with site size and habitat difficulty, from £3,110 up to £11,024, with bespoke pricing above 40ha.

Tewkesbury Borough Council

Invalid-application administration charges (agreed at Executive Committee on 20 March 2024, reviewed annually) — 2026/27 figures: £205 for major development, £105 for non-major, £55 for householder and all other development.

These apply if an application is made invalid and a satisfactory response isn't received within 28 days, or if an invalid application is withdrawn after being notified as invalid; the charge is deducted from any refund due. Core application fees from 1 April 2026 follow the national fee schedule.

Cheltenham Borough Council

Cheltenham's planning fees and charges are being confirmed. In the meantime, please contact Cheltenham Borough Council directly.
