School admissions

Applying for a primary, junior or secondary school place, key deadlines, appeals and in-year admissions.

This is a county-wide service, run once for the whole of Gloucestershire (previously by Gloucestershire County Council). It works the same wherever you live in the county.

The Co-ordinated Admissions team manages the statutory process for admitting children to primary (reception), junior (year 3) and secondary (year 7) schools, plus out-of-county transfers.

You must apply through the council — even if a sibling already attends, or you've "put your child's name down" at a school. That doesn't count as an application, and every year some families miss the deadline believing it does.

Primary / junior (reception and year 3)

Applications open in November and close 15 January for September entry the same year. Apply online 24/7, or by paper form (01452 425407). Decisions are announced 16 April, with a reply deadline of 23 April.

List five preferred schools in genuine preference order — under-listing is a real risk: name only one or two and, if you don't get in, you could be allocated a school far from home. An equal ranking system applies (schools don't see how you ranked them); it isn't first-come-first-served. If you want your catchment school, you must actually list it.

Secondary / high school (year 7)

Application window: September to 31 October the year before entry. Decisions announced 2 March, reply deadline 16 March.

Grammar schools: register separately for the Gloucestershire Grammar Schools' Entrance Test (2027 intake window given as noon 18 May 2026 to noon 26 June 2026 — confirm current-year dates). Passing the test is not an automatic offer — you must still list the grammar school(s) on the standard secondary form by 31 October. The admissions team doesn't see individual test results; those come from the grammar schools directly.

Waiting lists are requested via the Secondary Reply Form (not the online Family Portal). Accepting an offered place doesn't affect your waiting-list or appeal position for a preferred alternative — the council recommends accepting, as it guarantees a place while you pursue other options.

Appeals

There's a right of appeal for any refused preference. Academy and foundation schools handle their own appeals — contact the school. Community schools (Archway School is the only one in Gloucestershire; the rest are academies or foundation schools) go through school.admissions@gloucestershire.gov.uk. Each school sets its own appeal deadline.

Applying mid-year (in-year admissions)

For moves outside the normal round, apply directly to the preferred school(s) using an in-year admission form, not the co-ordinated process. Schools must respond within 10–15 school days and notify the council within 2. A refusal carries a right of appeal (the council assists alongside the school). Appeals team: 01452 426015.

School transport

If your allocated school qualifies for transport assistance, apply separately and early — the council needs several months' notice (application deadline around 31 May for a September start; confirm current-year date). Apply as soon as the allocation is known.

Delayed entry for summer-born children

Children born 1 April–31 August can request delayed entry to reception, starting the September after their fifth birthday. Requests must be made by the end of September after the child's fourth birthday. Governed by the DfE School Admissions Code 2021.

Co-ordinated Admissions Team: 01452 425407. Appeals: 01452 426015 (community schools) or the individual school (academies/foundation schools).
