# Universal Credit and Housing Benefit

## Universal Credit

Universal Credit is a single monthly payment for people of working age on a low income or out of work. It's gradually replacing six benefits and tax credits: Housing Benefit, Income Support, income-based Jobseeker's Allowance, income-related Employment and Support Allowance, Child Tax Credit, and Working Tax Credit. You generally can't get any of these separately once you're on Universal Credit.

If you're already claiming one of the legacy benefits, the DWP will eventually send a Migration Notice letter telling you to claim Universal Credit — don't act until you receive it, since you won't be moved automatically. Once the letter arrives you have a fixed deadline (3 months from the date it was sent) to claim. Your existing benefits stop the moment you submit a Universal Credit claim, and you can't go back to the old benefit afterward, so it's worth being sure before claiming.

Universal Credit is claimed and managed entirely online, and is administered nationally by the DWP, not the council — the council's role is limited to related local support (see below) and Council Tax Support, which has to be claimed separately.

## Housing Benefit

Most working-age people now claim help with rent through Universal Credit's housing element rather than Housing Benefit directly. Housing Benefit remains available for specific groups outside Universal Credit — broadly, people who've reached Pension Credit qualifying age, and people in certain types of supported or temporary accommodation.

**Local Housing Allowance (LHA)**, which applies if you rent privately, sets your maximum Housing Benefit based on: the Broad Rental Market Area (BRMA) you live in, the number of bedrooms your household needs, and the shared room rate for applicants under 35. Bedroom entitlement: one bedroom per single adult or adult couple, plus one more for any other adult aged 16+, any two children under 10, any two children of the same sex under 16, or any other child. If you rent from a housing association or council, Housing Benefit is instead based on your actual weekly eligible rent, which can be reduced if you have a spare bedroom (the "bedroom tax" — see GOV.UK for detail).

**Payment method** — you can have Housing Benefit paid to your own bank account, direct to a housing association landlord if you request it, or direct to a private landlord in specific vulnerability circumstances (learning difficulties, medical or mental health conditions, inability to open a bank account, debt problems, a history of arrears, undischarged bankruptcy, or previous evictions) under each council's Local Housing Allowance Safeguard Policy.

**Backdating** — if you weren't able to claim immediately, ask about backdating; this is assessed case by case.
