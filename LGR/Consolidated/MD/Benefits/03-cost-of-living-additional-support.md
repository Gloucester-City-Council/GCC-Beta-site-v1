# Cost of living and additional support

<!-- common -->
## Check what you're entitled to

An estimated £15bn in benefits goes unclaimed in the UK each year, and as many as 7 million people may be missing out on income-related state benefits they're entitled to. A free benefits calculator (available via GOV.UK and Turn2Us, among others) is the standard starting point, alongside a one-to-one check with a local Citizens Advice branch. If you're turned down for a benefit, it's worth getting advice on whether to challenge the decision — a meaningful proportion of benefit decisions are overturned on appeal or reconsideration.

## Pension Credit

If you're over State Pension age, Pension Credit tops up weekly income and unlocks access to further help — including help with housing costs, Council Tax, heating bills, and (for those 75+) a free TV licence. Around a third of eligible pensioners don't claim it, usually because they don't realise they're entitled or don't know they need to apply. You can have modest savings, some retirement income, or own your home and still qualify — it isn't a strict means test in the way some benefits are. Check eligibility and estimate the amount via the national Pension Credit calculator on GOV.UK. Pensioners receiving Pension Credit also get an automatic top-up to the Winter Fuel Payment, called the Pensioner Cost of Living Payment.

## Illness and disability benefits

Personal Independence Payment (PIP, working-age adults), Attendance Allowance (State Pension age), and Disability Living Allowance (children under 16, for new claims) are all non-means-tested — eligibility is based on how a health condition affects daily living and mobility, not on income or savings. Attendance Allowance in particular can be spent on anything (taxis to appointments, help around the house, bills) and doesn't affect your State Pension or ability to keep working. Getting one of these can increase entitlement to other benefits, including Pension Credit and Council Tax Support (CTR — see `council-tax-md/05-council-tax-reduction.md`). If you're already claiming DLA as an adult, get advice before claiming PIP instead — the DLA award stops the moment you do. If you're too unwell to work, New Style Employment and Support Allowance and/or Universal Credit with Limited Capability for Work (or Work-Related Activity) may apply. A separate Disability Cost of Living Payment is available to people already on a qualifying disability benefit.

## Carer's Allowance

If you provide at least 35 hours of care a week for someone, you may be entitled to Carer's Allowance and/or the carer element within Universal Credit — Carer's Allowance itself is currently around £69.70 a week where the eligibility criteria (yours, the cared-for person's, and the type of care given) are met.

## Debt and money advice

A consistent message across all five councils: seek advice early rather than letting debt problems build up. National organisations signposted everywhere include StepChange and National Debtline. Local Citizens Advice branches cover multiple districts jointly (Stroud & Cotswold share one branch), alongside local charities like P3 (financial skills, debt, education, employment support) and Clean Slate.

## Domestic abuse

If you or someone you know is experiencing domestic abuse, the Gloucestershire Domestic Abuse Support Service (01452 726 570, gdass.org.uk) covers the whole county. In a life-threatening emergency, call 999 (or text 999 if you can't speak safely).

**Gloucester City Council.** Council Tax Support (CTR — see `council-tax-md/05-council-tax-reduction.md` for eligibility detail): apply online or call 01452 396396. Low-income benefit/tax credit recipients may separately be eligible for a Cost of Living Payment (referenced at £650, paid in two instalments of £326 and £324 — treat this figure as time-sensitive and check the current rate before publishing, cost of living payment amounts change between years). Gloucester Citizens Advice: 01452 527202.

<!-- council:cotswold -->
**Cotswold District Council.** Council Tax Hardship Fund available to residents already receiving Council Tax Support (CTR — see `council-tax-md/05-council-tax-reduction.md`) whose circumstances are exceptional — a genuinely local, discretionary top-up distinct from statutory CTR itself. Uses the shared Stroud & Cotswold Citizens Advice branch (see Stroud, above) alongside the same national debt advice signposting.

<!-- council:forest-of-dean -->
**Forest of Dean District Council.** North and West Gloucestershire Citizens Advice provides one-to-one benefit-check support. Additional support signposted specifically: the Royal British Legion's Everyday Needs Grant for the Armed Forces community (up to £2,400 over 12 months, covering veterans and families), and RSPCA cost-of-living advice for struggling pet owners — the only one of the five councils to signpost pet-related cost-of-living support in the captured content.
