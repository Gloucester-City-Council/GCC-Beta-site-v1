# Discounts

<!-- common -->
## Single person discount

If you're the only adult living in the property, the bill is reduced by 25%. This is a national entitlement, set the same way everywhere — it isn't a local decision.

## Properties left empty and unfurnished

The default position in law is a 50% discount when nobody lives in a property. Councils have the power to reduce that discount, including down to nothing, for furnished-but-unoccupied and unfurnished-and-unoccupied properties (the s.11A power). Most councils have used that power. What's below is what each council has actually set.
<!-- /common -->

<!-- council:gloucester -->
Unoccupied and unfurnished: 25% discount for up to 6 months from when the furniture was removed. After 6 months, full charge applies — no discount.

## Major repair or structural work

A property that needs major repair or structural alteration to be habitable can qualify for a discount for up to 12 months while uninhabitable. What counts as "major" is set in law — roof structure repairs, rebuilding external walls or chimney stacks, foundation repair, replacing defective floor or ceiling joists, gutting most internal walls, or repairs after flood, fire or subsidence. Kitchen/bathroom refurbishment, general repairs, redecoration, rewiring on its own, and damp-proofing don't qualify.
<!-- /common -->

<!-- council:gloucester -->
Gloucester's major repair discount is 25%, for up to 12 months.
<!-- /council:gloucester -->

<!-- council:stroud -->
Stroud's major repair discount (Class D) is 25%, for up to 12 months.
