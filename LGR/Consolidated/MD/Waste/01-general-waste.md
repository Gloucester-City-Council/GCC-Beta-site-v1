# General waste

<!-- common -->
General waste is for rubbish that can't be recycled or composted. It's collected every two weeks everywhere in Gloucestershire, on the opposite week to recycling.

The same things are kept out of general waste everywhere, for the same reasons: garden waste and anything recyclable at the kerbside (it belongs in the other bins, not here); liquids like paint and oil (illegal to bin, and costly to clean up if spilled); building or DIY waste, rubble, soil, plasterboard (take to a Household Recycling Centre — plasterboard specifically can't go to landfill); trade waste, even if you run a business from home; hot ashes (let them cool first); sharp objects like needles or broken glass (double-wrap in paper, put in a rigid container first).

If a property can't take a wheeled bin — flats, mainly — councils issue refuse sacks instead, usually after an assessment.

Extra capacity beyond the standard bin is generally only available for larger households (commonly five-plus people) or on medical grounds, assessed case by case.
<!-- /common -->

<!-- council:gloucester -->
**Gloucester City Council.** Black bin or council-issued domestic sack. Out by 7am on collection day; remove from the highway by end of day. Replacement refuse sack £5, small bin £16.50, standard bin £25, large bin (where authorised) £55. Flats without communal facilities (typically 6 or fewer) get a 140L bin per flat. Contact: 01452 396 396.
<!-- /council:gloucester -->

<!-- council:stroud -->
**Stroud District Council.** Grey wheelie bin or beige bags, collected every two weeks, opposite week to recycling. Present by 6am. Not accepted: cling film, bubble wrap, polystyrene, crisp packets, sanitary products, nappies, pet waste (must be bagged), vacuum dust, rubble, garden waste/soil, batteries/vapes (these go weekly with food waste instead), clinical/hazardous waste, DIY materials, electrical goods, business waste, car parts, furniture. No smaller or bigger grey bin option — the council has decided the cost of swapping bins as household sizes change isn't sustainable. Residual waste goes to the Javelin Park Waste to Energy facility. Replacement bin: customer.services@stroud.gov.uk or 01453 766321.
<!-- /council:stroud -->

<!-- council:cotswold -->
**Cotswold District Council.** Grey wheelie bin, collected every two weeks. Out by 7am (6am during exceptional hot-weather early starts). Not accepted: stones/gravel/rubble, garden waste/soil, sharp objects, hot ashes, DIY waste, carpet and underlay, trade or building waste. Extra rubbish: beige sacks, 84p each, from council offices. Extra bin or sacks possible for households of more than 5, or with children in nappies.
<!-- /council:cotswold -->

<!-- council:forest-of-dean -->
**Forest of Dean District Council.** Black wheelie bin, collected every two weeks, out by 7am, lid closed; rubbish can be loose or bagged inside the bin, but no sacks left beside it. Accepted in general waste (despite not being recyclable elsewhere): nappies and sanitary products, polystyrene, crisp/sweet packets, bagged dog/cat waste, foil-lined food pouches, plastic films. Not accepted: garden waste, anything kerbside-recyclable (including food waste), liquids, trade waste, building/DIY waste/rubble/soil/plasterboard, small amounts of household asbestos (book in advance for the Recycling Centre instead), hot ashes. Flats unsuited to a wheelie bin get 80 beige refuse sacks a year, or a communal bin, following assessment. Extra beige sacks: £10 a roll. Extra bin eligibility: household of 6+, medical reasons, or large household with children in nappies — apply via 01594 810000.
<!-- /council:forest-of-dean -->

<!-- council:tewkesbury -->
**Tewkesbury Borough Council.** Green refuse bin, collected every two weeks, out by 7am. From mid-September 2025, new and replacement bins are 140 litres (down from 180L) — part of a "small on waste, big on recycling" policy approved by the Executive Committee on 4 September 2025, after county-wide analysis found roughly a quarter of household waste was food and another 23% was recyclable plastic, paper and card. Not accepted: batteries, vapes (supermarkets/HRCs instead), stones/gravel/rubble, garden waste/soil, sharp objects, DIY waste, carpet/underlay, commercial waste, paint/chemicals, clinical waste. Extra 140L bin: chargeable for households of 5+ who've maximised recycling; free on medical grounds. No additional bins issued for nappy waste — the Gloucestershire Real Nappy Project offers a £30 voucher towards reusable nappies instead. Clinical waste: yellow sharps boxes (from GP or pharmacy), returned sealed to a participating surgery or pharmacy.

On the narrow-access round (smaller vehicles, for properties Ubico and the council have assessed as needing them), general waste goes in a reusable black sack — rubbish must be bagged inside it, not loose, since crews pull it out by hand.
