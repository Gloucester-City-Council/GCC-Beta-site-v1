# Garden waste

<!-- common -->
Garden waste collection is an opt-in, paid subscription service everywhere in Gloucestershire — it isn't covered by the standard council tax-funded waste service. Subscriptions typically run on an annual cycle (commonly 1 April to 31 March), collected fortnightly through the growing season, and councils generally state there's no refund for cancelling partway through, or for missed individual collections, since payment doesn't guarantee a specific number of collections.

Compostable material accepted is broadly consistent: grass cuttings, hedge/shrub clippings, plants, leaves, twigs and small branches, weeds. Garden waste processed in Gloucestershire generally goes to open windrow composting (shredded, piled into windrows, turned over roughly ten weeks), with some compost used to restore old landfill sites and the remainder sold to farmers.

What's universally excluded: food waste (see the food waste page for why), soil and rubble in any quantity, plastic bags/pots/seed trays, animal faeces and bedding, chemicals/pesticides/herbicides, invasive weeds like Japanese knotweed and ragwort.

<!-- council:stroud -->
**Stroud District Council.** Two options: a 180-litre brown wheeled bin subscription at £58.50, or a bag subscription (for narrow-access properties a bin-lift vehicle can't reach) at £48 for 2026 — a reduced rate this year reflecting the later start date. Bag subscribers get 50 compostable sacks total (25 upfront, the remaining 25 once down to 6), can present up to 2 sacks per collection, and must pre-book each slot. Bin subscribers can buy more than one bin. Season: February to end of November, fortnightly. Renewal due each February. Bin tags applied by crews are a visual aid only, not mandatory — collections continue even if a bin isn't yet tagged. Lost or stolen bins aren't replaced free of charge.
<!-- /council:stroud -->

<!-- council:cotswold -->
**Cotswold District Council.** Annual licence required, displayed on the bin; non-transferable if you move (must be left at the property). Council Tax and Housing Benefit claimants get a 50% licence fee reduction if they provide their benefit reference number at sign-up (not retrospectively for claims made after signing up). Service paused 2 weeks over Christmas/New Year. **Forest of Dean District Council.** Annual licence, 1 April to 31 March, displayed beneath the bin handles. Sacks available (50 per licence) where a bin can't be accommodated, following assessment. Service can't be used by a gardener or grounds maintenance company on a householder's behalf. Bins remain council property. Suspended over Christmas/New Year with no fixed end date given in the source content (Cotswold's near-identical terms specify "2 weeks" — confirm whether Forest of Dean matches).

<!-- council:tewkesbury -->
**Tewkesbury Borough Council.** "Garden waste club" — membership year runs 1 April to 31 March, brown wheelie bin, fortnightly. No branded stickers any more; residents are asked to write their address on the bin in marker pen instead so crews can identify it. 14-day cooling-off period after signing up; refunds outside that window are at the council's discretion. No collections over the two-week Christmas/New Year period. Garden waste is processed by Open Windrow Composting at the Enovert site next to Wingmoor Farm HRC near Bishop's Cleeve — about a third of the compost is used to restore old landfill on site, the rest sold to farmers. Contact: recycling@tewkesbury.gov.uk, 01684 295010.
