# Recycling

<!-- common -->
Every council separates recycling into glass, paper/card, and plastics/tins/cans/foil at the point of collection — but the container types, colours, and exact frequency are set locally, so check your council's section below rather than assuming.

A few things hold everywhere: recycling generally needs to be loose, not bagged in plastic, when it goes in a box (bagged recycling usually isn't picked up — it can't be sorted that way); aerosols must be empty before recycling, for fire-safety reasons at the sorting facility; small electrical items are increasingly collected at the kerbside (in a bag, separate from the main recycling), but large electricals, light bulbs and fluorescent tubes still need a Household Recycling Centre.
<!-- /common -->

<!-- council:gloucester -->
**Gloucester City Council.** Two green recycling boxes (one for glass only; one for plastic, cans, tins, aerosols, foil and cartons together), plus a blue sack for all paper and card. Collected on the same cycle as general waste, opposite week. Since December 2019, glass must be kept separate from other recycling — contamination from broken glass was spoiling otherwise-good bales. Textiles, batteries, and anything in a carrier bag are no longer collected at the kerbside; batteries go to supermarkets instead. Small electrical items go loose on the lid of the green box. New containers and replacements are chargeable (council resolution, 11 February meeting); order via the council's container ordering page. Communal recycling applies to flats and excludes this kerbside-ordering route.
<!-- /council:gloucester -->

<!-- council:stroud -->
**Stroud District Council.** Green wheelie bin or green bag for cans, glass bottles/jars, plastic bottles, aerosols, foil, and drinks cartons together; a separate green recycling box for paper and card. Collected every two weeks, opposite week to general waste, out by 6am. Batteries and vapes now collected weekly alongside food waste rather than in the fortnightly recycling collection. Not accepted in the recycling stream: carrier bags/cling film, plastic toys, polystyrene, large plastic items, foil or glittered wrapping paper, kitchen roll, tissue paper, glass items other than bottles and jars (Pyrex, window glass, drinking glasses, light bulbs).
<!-- /council:stroud -->

<!-- council:cotswold -->
**Cotswold District Council.** Two black recycling boxes plus a white bag and a blue bag — genuinely the most subdivided scheme of the five: box one for glass bottles and jars; box two for paper; white bag for plastic bottles, tubs, pots, trays, tins, cans, aerosols, foil and cartons; blue bag for all cardboard. Small electricals, textiles and batteries each go in a separate old carrier bag tucked inside one of the boxes. Collected every two weeks, out by 7am (6am during exceptional hot-weather early starts). Not accepted: broken glass, drinking glasses, Pyrex, sheet/toughened glass, light bulbs; crisp packets, pet food pouches, cling film, bubble wrap, plastic film, coat hangers, takeaway coffee cups; car or motorbike batteries.
<!-- /council:cotswold -->

<!-- council:forest-of-dean -->
**Forest of Dean District Council.** Two green boxes plus a blue bag — green box one for plastic bottles/pots/tubs/trays/punnets, plant pots (except black), tins, cans, metal lids, empty aerosols, clean foil; green box two for glass bottles and jars (lids go in the tins-and-cans box instead) plus textiles in a tied carrier bag; blue bag for paper and card, including food-packaging card like pizza boxes if uncontaminated by food or grease. Collected weekly — the only one of the five councils collecting general recycling weekly rather than fortnightly. Out by 7am. Not accepted: hard plastics (toys, hangers, DVD cases), black plastics (the sorting facility's optical lasers can't detect black plastic), soft plastics/film, pet/baby food pouches, cartons (e.g. Tetra Pak — notably different from Gloucester and Stroud, who do accept cartons), polystyrene.
<!-- /council:forest-of-dean -->

<!-- council:tewkesbury -->
**Tewkesbury Borough Council.** Blue bin for card/cardboard, cartons, metal packaging (tins, cans, foil, aerosols), glass, and mixed paper together; collected fortnightly, out by 7am. Plastic containers and packaging (bottles, pots, tubs, trays) go in the same fortnightly collection. Accepts black plastic packaging, unlike Forest of Dean. Does not accept plastic bags/film, crisp packets, pre-prepared salad bags, polystyrene trays, or batteries/vapes (these go to supermarkets or HRCs). Small electricals go loose in an old carrier bag next to the blue bin, untied — not available on the narrow-access round, where vehicles don't have room for the collection cage.

On the narrow-access round: four reusable blue sacks for recycling, presented loose (not bagged inside another bag) — the opposite convention to the boxed schemes elsewhere, so don't assume "loose recycling" rules transfer directly between councils.
