# Food waste

<!-- common -->
Food waste is collected weekly everywhere — this is the one collection where all five councils run the same frequency, even though the recycling and general waste cycles differ. A small kitchen caddy is for day-to-day use indoors; it gets emptied into a larger outdoor caddy or bin for collection.

Food waste must never go in the garden waste bin. The reason is consistent across every council: garden waste is composted in the open air, and contamination from food or animal waste risks spreading bacteria that caused outbreaks like BSE and foot-and-mouth disease — open windrow composting can't safely process it the way an anaerobic digester can. Collected food waste is generally sent to anaerobic digestion, producing biogas (fed into the gas grid) and a fertiliser by-product spread on local farmland.

Pet waste, animal bedding, and animal carcasses are excluded from food waste collection everywhere, for the same biosecurity reason. Nappies, packaging, metal and glass are also universally excluded.

<!-- council:stroud -->
**Stroud District Council.** Grey food bin, sealed, plus an indoor kitchen caddy; collected weekly on the same day as refuse or recycling. Can line with plastic bags, bin liners, biodegradable bags or newspaper. Small batteries and vapes are now also collected weekly alongside food waste. Accepted: all cooked and raw food, meat and fish bones, pet food, tea bags, coffee grounds, food packaging. Not accepted: animal waste, pet bedding, nappies, garden waste.
<!-- /council:stroud -->

<!-- council:cotswold -->
**Cotswold District Council.** Kitchen caddy, emptied weekly. Accepted: uneaten food and plate scrapings, mouldy or out-of-date food, eggshells, small amounts of liquid/oil/fat including solid fats, tea bags, coffee grounds, newspaper or approved compostable liners, cat and dog food, animal hair, used kitchen roll and tissues. Not accepted: plastic (except carrier/food/sandwich bags used as caddy liners), any packaging, metal, glass, other household waste, animal faeces, animal bedding, pet litter, pet/animal carcasses, cardboard, nappies.
<!-- /council:cotswold -->

<!-- council:forest-of-dean -->
**Forest of Dean District Council.** Two containers: a 7-litre kitchen caddy and a 23-litre lidded outdoor bin, the largest outdoor capacity of the five councils' published figures. Collected weekly, out by 7am. Can line with plastic bags, compostable liners or newspaper. Accepted: fish, meat and bones (raw or cooked), dairy, hard fats and lard, tea bags, coffee grounds, fruit and veg peelings, cereals, bread, pastries, rice, pasta, stale/out-of-date food (packaging removed), uneaten pet food, plate scrapings, kitchen towels. Not accepted: packaging/pots/punnets/film, large quantities of oil, dead animals, garden waste, animal bedding and litter, animal/pet faeces.
<!-- /council:forest-of-dean -->

<!-- council:tewkesbury -->
**Tewkesbury Borough Council.** Kitchen caddy lined with compostable liners, paper, or old carrier/bread/sandwich bags; emptied weekly, dishwasher-proof. Accepted: all uneaten food and plate scrapings, mouldy or out-of-date food, eggs and eggshells, small amounts of liquids/oil/fat including solid fats, tea bags, coffee grounds, cat and dog food, used kitchen roll and tissues, meat and fish bones, fruit stones. Not accepted: plastic other than the bag types used as liners, any packaging (including foil trays), metal and glass, other household waste, animal faeces, animal bedding, pet litter, pet/animal carcasses, cardboard, nappies.
<!-- /council:tewkesbury -->
