# Long-term empty homes premium

<!-- common -->
A property that's been empty and substantially unfurnished for a long time can be charged an extra premium on top of the normal bill, to push owners toward bringing it back into use. The premium is attached to the property, not the owner — if you buy or rent a property that's already attracted the premium, you inherit the charge.

The maximum premiums allowed in law:

- Empty 1–5 years: up to 100% extra (double the normal bill)
- Empty 5–10 years: up to 200% extra (triple)
- Empty 10+ years: up to 300% extra (quadruple)

Whether a council has adopted these maximums, and from what date, is a local decision. All five Gloucestershire councils have adopted the full set of rates below — the difference is the date the 1-year threshold dropped in (it used to be 2 years).

## Exceptions — these classes don't get the premium

Same classes, same conditions, everywhere:

- **Class E** — would be someone's main home if they weren't living in job-related armed forces accommodation
- **Class F** — an annexe forming part of the main dwelling
- **Class G** — actively marketed for sale (12-month limit)
- **Class H** — actively marketed for let (12-month limit; from 1 May 2026 this expressly includes assured tenancies, following the abolition of assured shorthold tenancies)
- **Class I** — probate recently granted on a previously-exempt property (12 months from grant)
- **Class M** — undergoing major repair or structural alteration (12-month limit)

After the exception period ends, the property falls back into the premium unless a fresh qualifying transaction or tenancy resets the clock (Classes G, I and M).

<!-- council:gloucester -->
**Gloucester City Council.** From 1 April 2025: 100% premium once empty 1 year or more (previously the threshold was 2 years, set from 1 April 2020). 200% once empty 5 years or more (from 1 April 2020). 300% once empty 10 years or more (from 1 April 2021). Decision taken at full council, 7 February 2024.
<!-- /council:gloucester -->

<!-- council:stroud -->
**Stroud District Council.** From 1 April 2025: 100% premium once empty 1 year or more (previously 2 years, set from 1 April 2020). 200% once empty 5 years or more, 300% once empty 10 years or more — unchanged from the 2020/2021 determinations. Decision taken at full council, 22 February 2024 (original 2-year threshold set 19 December 2019).

Note: the discount/premium clock attaches to the property. If liability changes hands — sale or new tenancy — the start date for any discount used doesn't reset.
<!-- /council:stroud -->

**Cotswold District Council.** From 1 April 2024: 100% premium for 1–5 years empty, 200% for 5–10 years, 300% for 10+ years. Cotswold moved to the 1-year threshold a year earlier than the other four.
