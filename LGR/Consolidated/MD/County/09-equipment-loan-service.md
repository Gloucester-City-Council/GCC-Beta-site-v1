# Gloucestershire Equipment Loan Service (GELS)

One of GCC's own highest-contact-volume service categories, sitting within Adult Social Care but distinct enough in process to warrant separate coverage. GELS (previously known as GIS, and referred to elsewhere as the Community Equipment Service, CES) loans equipment to Gloucestershire residents to support independence at home, or to support carers. Jointly funded by GCC and local NHS partners.

## How you get equipment — you can't self-refer

This is not a service residents apply to directly. Equipment is supplied following an assessment of need by a healthcare professional, who submits the order. Once submitted, GELS's transport team contacts the resident directly to arrange delivery and fitting, working around the resident's or their support network's availability. There's no separate application form for the resident to complete for a first equipment request — the healthcare professional's referral is the trigger.

## What's covered

Mobility aids (walking frames, wheelchairs), bathing and toileting equipment, mattresses and beds, hoists, and general aids to daily living. Hoists, beds, bath lifts and air mattresses are on a formal breakdown and servicing contract with regular servicing built in. Slings issued with a hoist need to be made available for inspection by the service when requested.

## Legal basis

Delivered as part of Gloucestershire County Council's statutory duty under the Care Act 2014 to provide community equipment to people with assessed care and support needs — specifically supporting the Act's "prevent, reduce and delay" aims by helping people stay independent at home rather than needing more intensive care sooner. Logistics and distribution sit within GCC's Adult Social Care Directorate.

## Returning equipment

When equipment is no longer needed, arrange collection via the online form, or by phone/email. Returned equipment is professionally cleaned and, where possible, refurbished and reissued — GCC notes this is one of the harder parts of running the service, since getting equipment back promptly once someone stops needing it isn't always straightforward. Some items (bed pans, sock aids, and similar single-use-adjacent items) can't be returned or recycled at all.

## Sites

Two operational sites: the main site at Sudmeadow Road, Hempsted, Gloucester (warehouse storage plus some refurbishment), and a second site at Vantage Point, Mitcheldean, in the Forest of Dean, focused on refurbishment — replacing parts to original manufacturer specification (from raised toilet seats to bariatric beds) and keeping servicing current so equipment is safe to reissue.

## GELS as a supported employer

Beyond the equipment service itself, GELS operates as a supported business, offering paid trainee placements to people with significant disabilities as a route into sustainable employment — delivered in partnership with the Forwards Supported Employment Service (part of GCC's Employment and Skills Hub). Placements are 16 hours/week for 6 months, paid at National Living Wage, in production, transport, or administrative roles, with in-work support continuing after the placement ends. Eligibility follows Access to Work criteria: complex work-related support needs arising primarily from disability, support needs not resolvable through standard workplace adjustments alone, ability to work at least 16 hours/week with intensive support, and age 16+.
