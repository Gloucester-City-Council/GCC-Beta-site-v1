# Blue Badges and Concessionary Travel

One of GCC's own highest-contact-volume service categories, alongside Adult Social Care and Highways. Two distinct schemes: the Blue Badge (disabled parking) scheme, and concessionary bus travel (age-related and disability-related passes).

## Blue Badge — disabled parking

Lets eligible drivers or passengers with a disability or long-term health condition park closer to their destination, using certain restricted parking that would otherwise be off-limits. Applicants must be a Gloucestershire resident. Costs £10, refunded in full if the application is unsuccessful.

**Automatic qualification** (age 3+) applies to anyone receiving: the Higher Rate of the Mobility Component of Disability Living Allowance; the mobility component of PIP with 8+ points under "moving around"; or the mobility component of PIP with 10 points specifically for Descriptor E under "planning and following journeys" (inability to undertake any journey due to overwhelming psychological distress).

**Non-automatic (assessed) eligibility** covers an enduring (3+ years), substantial disability causing inability to walk, or considerable difficulty walking — including "very considerable psychological distress" — where this isn't self-evident from the form, an additional questionnaire may be required before a decision. A badge won't be issued to someone who only travels as a passenger or only struggles carrying shopping or luggage — the bar is about the applicant's own walking capability, not general convenience. Terminal illness with a life expectancy under 12 months (supported by a DS1500/SR1 medical form) is fast-tracked.

**What you need before applying:** digital copies of proof of identity (birth certificate, passport, driving licence), proof of address (Council Tax bill, government letter), proof of any relevant benefit (PIP/DLA/similar), and supporting evidence of the disability (GP or specialist diagnosis, prescribed medication list, GP patient summary — not appointment letters). Missing documents delay the application; insufficient evidence gets it rejected outright.

**Processing:** up to 12 weeks. If successful, the physical badge is sent by the Department for Transport, usually within 7–10 days of the decision letter, with delivery taking up to 21 days total. There's no formal appeal against a refusal, but you can request a review with new supporting evidence — a bare disagreement without new evidence won't be processed.

**Apply:** online, or by post to the Blue Badge team, Shire Hall, Westgate Street, Gloucester, GL1 2TG. Libraries can provide computer access if needed. Phone: 01452 425000 general enquiries.

## Concessionary bus passes

Free off-peak local bus travel anywhere in England, in one of two routes:

**Age-related** — qualifies at State Pension age (note: the eligibility calculator instructs everyone, regardless of sex, to select "female" when checking their date, since that produces the correct comparison date under current transitional rules). Can apply up to 28 days before becoming eligible. Apply online, or in person at a participating library (not all libraries participate — check first). Needs a recent colour digital passport-style photo (uploaded for online applications; taken on the spot at a library appointment). Contact: 01452 426265, or buspasses@gloucestershire.gov.uk.

**Disability-related** — available from age 5 for a defined list of conditions: a disability or injury with a substantial, long-term effect on walking ability; missing arms or long-term loss of use of both arms; and others, including anyone whose driving licence would be refused under section 92 of the Road Traffic Act 1988 on physical fitness grounds. A companion pass is available for anyone unable to travel alone because of their disability, letting one companion (16+) travel free alongside them. Evidence of eligibility is uploaded electronically for online applications; participating libraries can process it in person by appointment instead, with proof of disability and address brought along. Automatic renewal applies if the pass has been used in the last 13 months and GCC holds sufficient ongoing-eligibility information — otherwise a re-application letter goes out roughly a month before expiry. Contact: BusPasses2@gloucestershire.gov.uk for evidence forms.

**Replacing a lost, damaged or stolen pass:** £10 charge, free if stolen and a police crime reference number is provided. The old card is cancelled the moment a replacement is requested — there's a 5–7 working day gap before the new one arrives, so don't request a replacement speculatively.

**Liability note:** GCC is explicit that it isn't responsible for the bus operators who honour the scheme — using the pass is subject to that operator's own terms, and neither GCC nor other participating Gloucestershire authorities accept liability for anything arising from using an operator's service.

**Park & Ride discount:** a £1 return fare applies at Arle Court, the Racecourse, and Waterwells Park & Ride sites on production of a valid pass, after 9:30am on weekdays and any time at weekends/bank holidays — full fare applies before 9:30am on weekdays.

If you move address within Gloucestershire, you're required to notify the Integrated Transport Unit at Shire Hall in writing so pass records and renewal contact details stay current.
