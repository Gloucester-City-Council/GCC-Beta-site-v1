# Children, Young People and Families

Gloucestershire County Council is the statutory children's services authority for the county — responsible for safeguarding, social care, and support for children, young people and families. Ofsted currently rates the service 'Good' with 'Outstanding' elements.

## If you're worried about a child

**Children and Families Front Door Service:** 01452 426565, select option 2. Monday–Friday, 9am–5pm. This is the main route for reporting a welfare concern, or checking whether a child or family might be eligible for support.

**Outside office hours**, if it can't safely wait until the next working day, contact the Emergency Duty Team.

> GCC's own published contact page gives this number as **01452 614758**. A separate GCC safeguarding leaflet gives **01452 614194**. This is a genuine inconsistency in the council's own material, not a typo introduced here — check which is correct before this page goes live. Getting an emergency safeguarding number wrong is not a small mistake.

Both numbers reach an answering machine — leave a message and a number, and someone calls back. You don't have to give your name if you'd rather not.

**Childline:** 0800 1111 — free, confidential, for children and young people to contact directly.

If you've been hurt by anyone, or an adult or another young person has done something that's made you feel upset or scared, telling someone is the way to get support and protection. Everyone who works or volunteers with children has a responsibility to protect them from harm.

## For professionals

Referrals go through a Multi-Agency Referral Form (MARF) on the Liquid Logic portal — a one-time registration, no training needed. Where a child is at immediate risk of significant harm, contact the Front Door directly on 01452 426565 rather than relying on the portal alone.

A dedicated advice line (Monday–Friday, 8:30am–4:30pm) covers understanding Early Help and Social Care thresholds, talking through concerns to work out whether a MARF is needed, and support with MyPlans, the council's family support planning framework. This line replaced several separate advice lines — Community Social Worker, ECHO, and Family Information Service — which have since closed.

Concerns about a professional working with children go through a separate allegations management process, not the standard referral route.

## What's covered

- **Early help and targeted support**, delivered through Family Hubs and Youth Hubs under the Families First Partnership Programme
- **Family Information Service (FIS)** — signposting for families and young people aged 0–19, up to 25 for those with SEND, via the Glosfamilies Directory
- **Fostering and adoption**
- **Care leavers** support
- **Participation Team and Voice Gloucestershire** — the Children in Care Council, giving looked-after children a say in how services are run
- **Youth Justice Service**
- **Early Years Service**

Two documents set the strategic direction: the One Plan for children and young people in Gloucestershire (2024–2030), and the Home@theHeart Sufficiency Strategy (2025–2028), which sets out how the council plans to make sure enough local placements exist for children who can't live with their birth families.

## How information is shared

Gloucestershire County Council works with a range of partner organisations to coordinate support for children and families, and shares information where that's needed. The council says it will nearly always explain what's being shared and why before doing so — set out in full in the general privacy notice.
