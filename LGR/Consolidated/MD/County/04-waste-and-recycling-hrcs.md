# Household Recycling Centres

Gloucestershire County Council is the Waste Disposal Authority for the county — a different role to the district and city councils, who collect your bins at the kerbside as Waste Collection Authorities. GCC runs the county's Household Recycling Centres, branded Gloucestershire Recycles, in partnership with the district, city and borough councils.

## Book before you go

Every visit — car or van — needs a booking made in advance online. This isn't a suggestion; you can be turned away without one. It keeps queues and congestion down and makes the sites safer, and GCC's own customer feedback shows most people prefer it this way. With over 10,000 bookings a week across the five sites, there isn't capacity to take bookings by phone as standard. If you genuinely can't book online, and nobody can book on your behalf, there's a phone line: 01452 596 626, weekdays 9am–5pm.

Bookings run in fixed 30-minute slots. Arrive within yours — turning up outside it risks more vehicles showing up than the site can handle safely, and you may be turned away and asked to rebook. You'll need to give your name, email, postcode, vehicle make and model, registration, and what waste you're bringing. That information helps manage the booking system, lets the council contact you if a site closes unexpectedly, and helps prevent illegal commercial waste dumping. Bring your booking reference — printed or on your phone — to get in.

Need to change your vehicle or your slot? There are separate amendment forms for cars and for vans, pick-ups and large trailers — you'll need your booking reference and vehicle registration either way.

## What counts as a car visit

Passenger cars, optionally with a small trailer (under 6ft x 4ft internally, including any boxed-off sections). Anything bigger — a van, pick-up, or larger trailer — needs a separate van/large-vehicle booking. HRCs are for household waste only. Bringing commercial or trade waste is a criminal offence, and vehicle types are checked against council policy.

## What you can't bring

Fly-tipped waste isn't accepted — the sites aren't licensed for it. Waste fly-tipped on private land needs a private licensed disposal route instead. Bring only what genuinely can't go through kerbside collection or another disposal route.

## Sites

Five council-run sites across the county, plus one separate site at Swindon Road in Cheltenham, run independently by Cheltenham Borough Council and currently closed pending review.

Known sites: **Hempsted** (Gloucester) — due to close during 2026 for essential repairs and improvements, exact dates to be confirmed — **Pyke Quarry** (near Horsley, Stroud), and **Wingmoor Farm** (Bishop's Cleeve).

Each site closes one weekday a week — Tuesday or another midweek day, staggered across the county so there's always a reasonable alternative open. That pattern comes from usage data showing midweek is quietest, and it deliberately keeps sites open on Bank Holiday Mondays and Good Friday, historically the busiest days. All sites close on Christmas Day, Boxing Day and New Year's Day.

If you need help lifting your waste, ask the staff member at the entrance.

## Behaviour on site

GCC operates a zero-tolerance policy on violence, aggression, threats or harassment toward staff or other visitors. Anyone responsible loses access to the service.

## Contact

General enquiries: waste@gloucestershire.gov.uk. For booking issues specifically, use the amendment forms above, or the phone line if you can't book online.
