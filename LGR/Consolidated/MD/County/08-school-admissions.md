# School Admissions (Co-ordinated Admissions)

Gloucestershire County Council's Co-ordinated Admissions team manages the statutory process for admitting children to primary (reception), junior (year 3), and secondary (year 7) schools, plus out-of-county lower-to-middle and middle-to-high transfers. One of GCC's own highest-contact-volume service categories.

## Why you must apply through the county, even with a sibling already at the school

A genuinely common misunderstanding, called out directly in GCC's own guidance: having a child's name down at a preferred school, or an older sibling already attending, does **not** substitute for a formal application. Every year some parents miss the deadline because they believed this. You must apply to the county council for a place regardless.

## Primary/junior admissions (reception and year 3)

Applications open in November and close **15 January** (for September entry the same year). Apply online 24/7, or by paper form requested from Co-ordinated Admissions on 01452 425407. Allocation decisions are announced **16 April**, with a reply deadline of **23 April**.

List five preferred schools, in genuine preference order. GCC's guidance is explicit that under-listing is a real risk: if you only name one or two schools and don't get in, you could be allocated a school far from home with no say in which. Schools don't see the order they've been ranked in by other applicants — an equal ranking system applies, not first-come-first-served. If you want to be considered for your local/catchment school, you must actually list it — it isn't assumed by default.

## Secondary/high school admissions (year 7)

Application window: September through **31 October** of the year before entry. Allocation decisions announced **2 March**, reply deadline **16 March**.

**Grammar school route:** requires registering separately for the Gloucestershire Grammar Schools' Entrance Test (registration window given for the 2027 intake: noon 18 May 2026 to noon 26 June 2026 — confirm current-year dates before citing). Passing the entrance test is **not** an automatic offer — the grammar school(s) must also be listed on the standard secondary application form by the 31 October deadline, or no place will be offered regardless of test result. The Co-ordinated Admissions Team doesn't have access to individual entrance test results — that information comes from the grammar schools directly.

**Waiting lists:** requested via the Secondary Reply Form specifically (not the online Family Portal). Second-round waiting list closing date given as midnight 16 March, with outcomes notified by 6 April — confirm current-year dates. Accepting an offered place doesn't affect your waiting-list or appeal position for a preferred alternative, and GCC actively recommends accepting for this reason — it guarantees a place while you pursue other options.

## Appeals

Right of appeal exists for any refused preference. Academy and foundation schools handle their own appeals — contact the school directly. Community schools (Archway School is stated as the only community school in Gloucestershire; all others are academies or foundation schools) go through school.admissions@gloucestershire.gov.uk instead. Each school sets its own appeal deadline.

## Applying mid-year (in-year admissions)

For moves outside the normal admission round: apply directly to the preferred school(s) using an in-year admission form, not through the main co-ordinated process. Schools must respond within 10–15 school days and are required to notify the County Council of each application and outcome within 2 school days. A refusal carries a legal right of appeal, and GCC will assist with that process alongside the school. Appeals team contact: 01452 426015.

## School transport

If your child is allocated a school that qualifies for transport assistance, apply separately and early — GCC needs several months' notice to assess eligibility and arrange transport for a September start, with an application deadline around 31 May for that September (confirm current-year date). Applying as soon as the school allocation is known, rather than waiting, is explicitly recommended.

## Delayed entry for summer-born children

Children born 1 April–31 August in a given year ("summer-born") can request delayed entry to reception, starting the September after their fifth birthday instead of the standard year group. Requests must be made by the end of September after the child's fourth birthday — after that point GCC considers the child to have effectively been admitted, with a one-month trial period built in to help parents judge readiness. This is governed by the Department for Education's School Admissions Code 2021, applied via GCC's own updated admissions policy.

## Contact

Co-ordinated Admissions Team: 01452 425407. Appeals: 01452 426015 (community schools) or the individual school (academies/foundation schools).
