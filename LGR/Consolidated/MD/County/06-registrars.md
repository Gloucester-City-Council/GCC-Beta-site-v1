# Births, Marriages, Deaths and Civil Partnerships (Registration Service)

Gloucestershire County Council runs the county's statutory registration service — births, deaths, marriages, civil partnerships, citizenship ceremonies, and the underlying historical indexes. Everything is by pre-booked appointment; there's no walk-in registration.

## Registering a birth

Births can only be registered in the district where they occurred, though information can be given at any register office in England or Wales — details are then forwarded to the correct district. The baby doesn't need to attend. The hospital or local health authority notifies the registrar directly that a birth has taken place.

If the parents aren't married or in a civil partnership and want both parents' details on the certificate: either parent can register alone and bring a signed Statutory Declaration of Acknowledgment of Parentage from the other, or the mother can register without the father's details and add them later through re-registration.

A full birth certificate costs £12.50 — pre-payable online at booking, or payable by card or cash at the appointment. Certificates aren't issued on the day; they're posted from the district where the birth took place.

Gloucestershire also runs occasional outreach registration sessions at community locations (for example, a monthly session at Lydney River Children and Family Centre) — check current dates before relying on this, as they rotate. Note: at these outreach sessions, certificates can't be issued on the spot even where they normally could be at a main office.

To book: 01452 425060, option 2.

## Registering a death

**Legal deadline: within 5 days of the date of death**, including weekends and bank holidays, in the district where the death occurred. If the death has been referred to the coroner, registration has to wait until the coroner's investigation concludes.

A death can't be registered without a Medical Certificate of Cause of Death (MCCD), transmitted electronically from the doctor to the register office after review by a Medical Examiner (an independent senior doctor not involved in the person's care). Don't book an appointment until you've been told the MCCD has been sent through — the guidance is explicit that GPs sometimes hand the MCCD to the family directly, which is acceptable but means the register office hasn't had a chance to check it for errors first, which can cause delays at the appointment itself. Next of kin can book an appointment 24 hours after the MCCD is sent, to avoid exactly this kind of issue.

If someone unable to register in Gloucestershire (because the death occurred elsewhere) needs to give the details, another registration district can take the details and pass them through to Gloucestershire to complete the registration.

At the appointment (in a private room, typically no more than 40 minutes): bring anything supporting the details to be recorded — names, addresses, relationships, dates — though the absence of supporting documents doesn't stop registration going ahead. The deceased's occupation, and their spouse or civil partner's name and occupation, are needed if relevant, along with whether they received any government pension. If more than one person books an appointment to register the same death, only the first-scheduled appointment goes ahead; the rest are cancelled automatically.

At the end of the appointment you're given a reference number for the free Tell Us Once service — notifies multiple government and council departments in one go, usable online or by phone within 28 days, not compulsory. Death certificates cost £12.50 each, payable at booking or at the appointment.

To book: 01452 425060, option 1.

## Ordering certificates

Full certificates cost £12.50. A short birth certificate (no parent details) is available but isn't accepted by passport offices or many other organisations — check it's fit for purpose before ordering the cheaper version. A priority service exists for £38.50, issuing the next working day (not available weekends or bank holidays), sent Royal Mail 1st class. Certificates can also be collected in person from the Gloucestershire Heritage Hub in Gloucester rather than posted — uncollected certificates are posted automatically after 10 days. Online certificate orders have a 15-minute window to complete payment once started.

## Marriages, civil partnerships and citizenship ceremonies

Handled through a separate site, gloucestershireregistrationservice.co.uk, rather than the main GCC domain — this includes ceremony booking, approved premises licensing, and registering a building for worship and marriage. Citizenship ceremonies are booked the same way.

## Correcting errors and re-registration

Applications to re-register a birth (for example, following the parents' marriage, or to add a father's details) use specific government forms, submitted by post to Cheltenham Register Office.

## Contact routes

Phone: 01452 425060 — option 1 (death registration), option 2 (birth registration), option 3 (marriages and civil partnerships/ceremonies), option 4 (other enquiries).
Post: Gloucestershire Registration Service, St Georges Road, Cheltenham, Gloucestershire, GL50 3EW.
Data requests: grsdatarequest@gloucestershire.gov.uk, in writing.

Eight registration offices operate across the county (referenced across sources: Cheltenham, Gloucester — inside Shire Hall, appointment-only — Quedgeley, Cinderford, Stroud, Cirencester, Tewkesbury, and Moreton-in-Marsh).

## Policy and conduct

A published Safeguarding Policy and a Respect Notice (covering expected behaviour in register offices) both apply. Gloucester's Shire Hall office is explicit that arriving 10+ minutes late for a booked appointment means rebooking, since the slot can't be held against other bookings.

## Not covered by this service

Baby loss certificates (for pregnancy loss before 24 weeks) are a separate national scheme, applied for via GOV.UK directly — the registration service has no role in issuing them, and the GCC page is explicit about this to avoid residents contacting the wrong service.
