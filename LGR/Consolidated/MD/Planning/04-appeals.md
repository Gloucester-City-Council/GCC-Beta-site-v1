# Appeals

<!-- common -->
If you disagree with a decision on your own application, you can appeal to the Planning Inspectorate (acting for the Secretary of State) — but only on your own application; you can't appeal against someone else's. Grounds for appeal: your application was refused, you disagree with a condition attached to your permission, your application wasn't decided within the agreed timescale, or you're appealing an enforcement notice.

Appeal deadlines are set nationally and are the same everywhere:

- **8 weeks** from the decision date — advertisement consent decisions
- **12 weeks** from the decision date — householder applications
- **6 months** from the decision date — most other applications

Send your appeal to the Planning Inspectorate (online, in most cases) and send a copy to the council as well. The Planning Inspectorate's own guidance covers how to make an appeal in detail.

If your application is refused, the more direct route is often to go back to the council to discuss possible amendments and resubmit, rather than appeal — appeals take longer and aren't guaranteed to succeed.
