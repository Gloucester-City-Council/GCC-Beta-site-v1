# Fees and charges

<!-- common -->
There are two genuinely different kinds of fee here, and it's worth being precise about which is which — one is national, one isn't.

**The core planning application fee** — what you pay to have your application considered at all — is set by national government regulation. The amount depends on the type and scale of development, and it's the same fee for the same type of application in every council in England. This isn't something any of the five Gloucestershire councils has discretion to change. Use the Planning Portal fee calculator or your council's own calculator to work out the amount for your specific proposal.

**Administration charges for invalid applications** are a different thing entirely — a locally-determined charge for the cost of handling an application that's returned because it's incomplete or doesn't meet validation requirements. These genuinely differ between councils, both in amount and in how they're triggered. See each council's figures below.

**Section 106 and Biodiversity Net Gain fees** are also generally locally set (monitoring fees, registration charges), even where the underlying legal frameworks — Section 106 planning obligations, the national BNG requirement — are national.

**Stroud District Council.** £85 administration charge on planning applications submitted by email or hard copy. Applications that remain invalid after 21 days incur an additional £85 administration charge. Planning fees, pre-application advice fees, and BNG monitoring fees all increased from 1 April 2026 — check the current fee calculator rather than relying on a fixed figure here.
<!-- /council:stroud -->

<!-- council:cotswold -->
**Cotswold District Council.** £85 administration fee charged on all applications submitted by email or hard copy (as opposed to via the Planning Portal). A separate £30 fee applies to Regulation 77 applications (habitat/species mitigation near protected sites), set nationally by government rather than by the council.
<!-- /council:cotswold -->

<!-- council:forest-of-dean -->
**Forest of Dean District Council.** £30 administration charge for applications not submitted through the Planning Portal. Separate charges for handling invalid applications, effective from 1 April 2025: £55 for householder, advertisement, certificate of lawfulness and prior notification applications; £110 for minor applications; £220 for major applications — noticeably the steepest major-application charge of the councils captured. Section 106 registration charge: £540. Section 106 monitoring charges scale with dwelling numbers: £540 for under 10 units, £1,080 for 10–100 units, £5,380 for 101–250 units, £10,760 for 251+ units. BNG fees scale with site size and habitat difficulty, from £3,110 (small site, low difficulty) up to £11,024 (large site, high difficulty, 20–40ha) with bespoke pricing above 40ha.
<!-- /council:forest-of-dean -->

<!-- council:tewkesbury -->
**Tewkesbury Borough Council.** Invalid-application administration charges, agreed at Executive Committee on 20 March 2024 and reviewed annually — figures for 2026/27: £205 for major development, £105 for non-major development, £55 for householder and all other development. These apply if an application is made invalid and a satisfactory response isn't received within 28 days, or if an invalid application is withdrawn after being notified as invalid; the charge is deducted from any refund due. Core planning application fees effective from 1 April 2026 are published separately and follow the national fee schedule.
