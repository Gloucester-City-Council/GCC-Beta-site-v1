# Housing register and Homeseeker Plus

<!-- common -->
All social housing in Gloucestershire and West Oxfordshire is let through one shared scheme: **Homeseeker Plus**. This isn't five similar local schemes — it's one partnership between all six Gloucestershire councils, other participating authorities, and housing associations. Whichever of the five councils' websites you're reading this on, the underlying scheme, priority bands, and bidding rules are the same.

## How it works

A choice-based lettings system: rather than the council allocating you a property, you register, get assessed and banded, then actively bid on properties advertised on the Homeseeker Plus website that match your circumstances.

**Five steps:**
1. Register with Homeseeker Plus and complete the application form (roughly 30 minutes)
2. Get assessed — takes 4 to 6 weeks; you may be asked for supporting information
3. Receive your banding decision by email or letter, including your bedroom need and confirmed local connections
4. Browse adverts — new properties can be posted any weekday and normally stay live for 7 days
5. Bid — up to 3 live bids at a time; there's no money involved, you're just expressing interest

## Priority bands

Four bands, in order of priority: **Emergency**, **Gold**, **Silver**, **Bronze**. Full eligibility criteria for each band are set out in the Homeseeker Plus policy, common to all participating authorities.

## How shortlisting works

After bidding closes, the shortlist is reviewed and the property allocated based on: eligibility for that specific property, banding, and the date the banding was awarded (priority date) — never the time you placed your bid within the bidding window. Some adverts specify a Parish Local Connection requirement, stated explicitly if it applies. If your bid is successful, the housing association assesses you against their own tenancy criteria before offering a viewing. You can refuse an offer, but refusing three or more may see your application suspended, and there are further restrictions if you hold a homelessness banding specifically.

## Eligibility, savings and income

There's no fixed savings or income limit. Instead, if an assessment concludes you have enough savings or household income (no fixed threshold, but the FAQ content references circumstances around £60,000/year as a discussion point) to meet your housing needs on the open market, you may be judged ineligible for the register. Existing homeowners aren't normally eligible, though exceptions exist — severe negative equity or an unmeetable medical need are examples given.

## Transfers and swaps

Existing social tenants in Gloucestershire wanting to move can apply for a transfer through Homeseeker Plus, or search for a mutual exchange via HomeSwapper — free for most Gloucestershire tenants through county membership of the scheme, rather than the At the standard per-user fee others might pay elsewhere in the UK.

<!-- council:gloucester -->
**Gloucester City Council.** Contact: Homeseeker@gloucester.gov.uk. Gloucester also runs a specific **Move-on priority banding** route for people leaving supported/sheltered accommodation with care, support or supervision, and for care leavers — assessed once the supported accommodation provider confirms the applicant is ready for independent living, and once Gloucester accepts it's the appropriate authority to rehouse them. Under the Homeseeker Plus partnership, applicants living in supported housing outside their home authority area are reconnected to their home area once ready to move on. Applications aim to be assessed within 4 to 6 weeks. Gloucester applicants can also bid on new-build affordable homes in Tewkesbury Borough — delivered jointly with Cheltenham and Tewkesbury Borough councils, currently including Perrybrook (Brockworth), with Innsworth and Pirton Fields to follow — with an equal chance on those specific properties even without a local connection to Tewkesbury.
