# Second homes

<!-- common -->
A property that's furnished but isn't anyone's main home — used for weekends, holidays, or occasionally — is a second home. No discount applies to a second home; full council tax is payable as a baseline.

The Levelling-up and Regeneration Act 2023 gave councils the power to charge an additional premium on second homes, on top of the full charge. Adopting the premium, and at what percentage (up to 100%), is a local decision — it isn't automatic. All five Gloucestershire councils have adopted a 100% premium from 1 April 2025; the difference is the date each one resolved to do so.

## Exceptions — these don't get the second homes premium

- **Class E** — armed forces accommodation
- **Class F** — occupied annexe
- **Class G** — marketed for sale (12-month limit)
- **Class H** — marketed for let (12-month limit; includes assured tenancies from 1 May 2026)
- **Class I** — probate recently granted (12 months from grant)
- **Class J** — job-related second home
- **Class K** — caravan pitch or boat mooring
- **Class L** — planning condition preventing occupation for 28+ continuous days, or restricting use to holiday letting, or barring use as a sole/main residence

If you believe a property is actually a holiday let rather than a second home, council tax still applies at the normal rate until the Valuation Office Agency agrees to move it onto the business rates list instead.
