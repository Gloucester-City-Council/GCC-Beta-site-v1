About the council

Who we are, the area we serve, how we're run, and the story of how Gloucestershire's councils came together as one.

Gloucestershire Council is the new unitary authority for Gloucestershire. On 1 April 2028 — vesting day — it brought together Gloucestershire County Council and the six district and borough councils into a single council responsible for every local government service across the county, from adult social care and roads to bins, planning and council tax.

One council means one place to find services, one phone number to call, and one organisation accountable for getting things right — while keeping decisions as close as possible to the communities they affect.

We are still bringing everything together. While that happens, some services are still delivered through the former councils' websites — the tool on our home page asks where you live and takes you straight to the right one.

Why local government reorganised

For decades, Gloucestershire ran a two-tier system: a county council for services like education, social care and highways, and six district and borough councils for services like bins, housing and planning. It worked, but it also meant residents often weren't sure who to contact, and effort was duplicated across seven organisations.

Reorganisation brings those services together under one unitary council. The aim is simpler access for residents, lower running costs, and clearer accountability — with the savings reinvested in frontline services.

How the council works

Gloucestershire Council is made up of elected councillors, supported by council officers. Councillors set the policies and budget; officers deliver the day-to-day services.

Full Council

All elected councillors meet to agree the budget, the council's major plans, and the overall policy framework.

Leader & Cabinet

The Leader and a small Cabinet take most day-to-day decisions, each Cabinet member holding a specific portfolio.

Scrutiny committees

Cross-party committees hold the Cabinet to account, review decisions, and examine how services are performing.

Regulatory committees

Planning, licensing and audit committees take specific statutory decisions independently of political priorities.

Our priorities

Our Council Plan sets out what we're focused on for residents:

  • One council that's easier to deal with — services in one place, closer to communities
  • Value for money, with reorganisation savings reinvested in frontline services
  • A thriving local economy and vibrant town centres
  • Tackling climate change and protecting Gloucestershire's environment
  • Strong, healthy and connected communities

The road to one council

  1. 2024–2025

    Government invited Gloucestershire's councils to propose a single unitary authority, and the proposal was agreed.

  2. 2026

    Transition planning began, and residents had their say on services, priorities and the new council's name.

  3. May 2027

    Shadow elections — residents elected the councillors who formed the new authority and oversaw the final year of transition.

  4. 1 April 2028

    Vesting day — Gloucestershire Council formally went live. Gloucestershire County Council and the six district and borough councils were abolished.

  5. 2028 onwards

    Services are progressively brought onto one website, one phone line and one set of systems.

Budget and spending

Our budget is agreed each year by Full Council and funded through council tax, business rates, government grants and fees and charges. We publish our spending, contracts over £5,000, senior salaries and performance data as part of our commitment to transparency.

Reorganisation is expected to reduce running costs by removing duplication across the former seven councils — for example, one set of senior management, one payroll, and shared back-office systems.
