One council for Gloucestershire goes live in 2028

What vesting day means for residents — and what stays exactly the same.

← All news

Published 27 June 2026

Newstershire Council, the new single council for the whole of Gloucestershire, will formally take over local services on 1 April 2028 — known as vesting day. From that date, one council will be responsible for everything from adult social care and roads to bins, planning and council tax.

Until then, the current county, district and borough councils continue to run services as normal. Residents don't need to do anything — bins will be collected, bills will be issued, and services will run as usual through the changeover.

What's changing

Bringing seven councils together into one is intended to make services simpler to find and use. Over time, residents will have a single website, one phone number and one account for their council services, rather than needing to work out which organisation to contact.

What's staying the same

Day-to-day services continue without interruption. Where a service is delivered locally today, it will keep running — the change is about how the council is organised behind the scenes, not about stopping or reducing services.

We'll keep publishing updates here as the transition progresses.
