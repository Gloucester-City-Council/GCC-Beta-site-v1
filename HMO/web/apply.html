Apply for an HMO licence

Apply for a new or renewal HMO licence for a property in Gloucester.

Before you start

You can apply for an HMO licence if:

  • Your property is occupied by 5 or more people forming more than 1 household who share facilities such as kitchens or bathrooms.

You will also need to confirm that:

  • You are a fit and proper person — the licence holder must be a named individual. If a company wishes to licence a property, the licence holder must be a named person within the company.

What you will need

  • The full address of the property
  • Full details of all amenities and rooms in the property
  • A current Gas Safety Certificate (CP12)
  • A current Electrical Installation Condition Report (EICR)
  • A fire risk assessment for the common parts of the property

Fees

HMO licence fees from 1 April 2026
Licence type Duration Fee
New licence Up to 5 years £1,260.00
Renewal Up to 5 years £900.00

Warning: Operating an HMO without a licence is a criminal offence. You could face an unlimited fine or a civil penalty of up to £30,000. (Housing Act 2004, s.72; Housing and Planning Act 2016, ss.28–31)
