Operator licensing

Apply for a private hire operator licence. Find out about your responsibilities for staff checks, booking records and your operating base.

Who needs an operator licence

You need a private hire operator licence to accept bookings for private hire vehicles in Gloucester. This applies to companies and sole traders.

Your operating base must be within Gloucester City boundaries. A standard licence lasts 5 years. The council may grant a shorter licence at its discretion.

Fit and proper person assessments apply to all directors and partners of the business, not just the named licence holder.

DBS checks and staff requirements

Operator DBS check

If you are not also a licensed driver with Gloucester City Council, you must complete an annual Basic DBS check.

Booking and dispatch staff

All staff who take bookings or dispatch vehicles must have a Basic DBS check. You must define how often re-checks are carried out; the common standard is every 3 years.

Policy on employing ex-offenders

You must have a written policy on employing ex-offenders and on when re-checks are required. The policy must be available for inspection by the council.

Outsourced booking and dispatch

If you use a third-party provider for booking or dispatch, you are responsible for ensuring that provider meets the same DBS and policy standards.

Booking records

You must keep a record of every booking. Each record must include:

  • Passenger name
  • Time of request
  • Pick-up point
  • Destination
  • Driver name
  • Driver licence number
  • Vehicle registration number
  • Name of the person who took the booking
  • Name of the person who dispatched the vehicle

Retention and access

Records must be kept for at least 6 months from the date of the last entry. You must produce records to council officers or police on request.

Vehicle and driver records

You must also keep records of all vehicles you operate, including the owner and registration or plate number, and all drivers, including their name and badge number.

Booking and advertising rules

  • Fares for journeys outside Gloucester must be agreed in advance with the passenger
  • You must not use the words 'taxi' or 'cab' in your advertising
  • You may only use Gloucester City Council-licensed drivers and vehicles
  • You may sub-contract work to other licensed operators under the Deregulation Act 2015

Complaints and enforcement

All licensed vehicles must display information about how passengers can complain, including the council's contact details, the plate number and the website address.

Sanctions range from written warnings to suspension or revocation of your licence, or prosecution, depending on the seriousness and history of the matter.

Right of appeal

You have 21 days to appeal a licence decision to the Magistrates' Court. Note that the right of appeal does not apply to immediate vehicle suspension under section 68 of the Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1976.
