Driver licensing

Apply for or renew a hackney carriage or private hire driver licence. Both licences cover you to drive either type of vehicle.

Who needs a driver licence

You must hold a Gloucester City Council driver licence if you drive a hackney carriage or private hire vehicle in Gloucester for payment. This applies to both new applicants and anyone renewing an existing licence.

A licence lasts up to 3 years. The council may grant a shorter licence at its discretion.

Eligibility

To be considered for a licence, you must:

  • Be aged 21 or over
  • Hold a full UK driving licence for at least 12 months
  • Have no more than 3 penalty points on your DVLA licence if you are a new applicant
  • Pass a fit and proper person assessment
  • Have the right to work in the United Kingdom

If you have lived abroad

If you have lived outside the UK, you must provide your addresses for the past 5 years. For each country where you lived for more than 6 months since age 18, you must provide a certificate of good conduct. If the certificate is not in English, you must provide a certified translation.

Student visa applications are not accepted.

Checks and clearances

Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) check

You need an Enhanced DBS check covering the Other Workforce and both the Child and Adult Barred Lists. This check must be renewed every 6 months. You will need to subscribe to the DBS Update Service to allow us to check your status quickly.

National register check

All new and renewal applications are checked against the National Register of Taxi Licence Refusals and Revocations (NR3). Any refusals or revocations of your licence will be recorded on this register.

Medical assessment

You need a Group 2 medical certificate. The certificate must be no more than 3 months old when you apply.

  • From age 45: renew every 5 years
  • From age 65: renew every year

Assessments and training

Driving assessment

You must pass a driving assessment with an approved provider. You will need to retake this assessment every 10 years.

Knowledge test

You must pass a knowledge test covering licensing regulations and topographical knowledge of Gloucester.

You must pass a basic literacy and numeracy test.

Safeguarding training

Safeguarding training is mandatory for all new applicants. You must complete a refresher every 3 years.

English language test

You must demonstrate sufficient English language skills unless you provide an appropriate certificate of English proficiency.

Your duties as a licensed driver

Once licensed, you must follow the code of conduct at all times when driving a licensed vehicle.

  • Wear your licence badge so it is clearly visible at all times
  • Be polite and punctual
  • Assist passengers with luggage and help disabled passengers
  • Take the shortest or most economical route unless the passenger agrees otherwise
  • Keep the vehicle smokefree at all times, including vaping
  • Do not play loud music; turn off the radio if asked
  • Carry guide and assistance dogs unless you have a medical exemption
  • Provide a receipt on request
  • Only use a mobile phone hands-free while driving
  • Maintain a clean and tidy appearance

Assistance dogs

You must carry guide dogs, hearing dogs and other assistance dogs. You cannot charge extra. You can only refuse if you hold a medical exemption certificate from the council.

Your duty to report incidents

You must tell the council within 48 hours if any of the following happen:

  • You are arrested, released under investigation, charged or convicted of any sexual, dishonesty or violence offence
  • You receive any conviction, caution or fixed penalty notice
  • You have a pending court case
  • You receive any motoring offence

If you are arrested or interviewed under caution, you must also tell the police that you are a licensed driver.

Enforcement and appeals

The council operates a penalty points system. If you accumulate more than 12 points in any 12-month period, your case will be referred to the Licensing and Enforcement Sub-Committee for review.

In serious cases, the council may suspend or revoke your licence immediately to protect public safety.

Right of appeal

If the council refuses, suspends or revokes your licence, you have 21 days to appeal to the Magistrates' Court (or Crown Court where applicable).
