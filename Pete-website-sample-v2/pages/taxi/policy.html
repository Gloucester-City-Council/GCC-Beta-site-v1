Policy and guidance

The legislative framework, policy documents, rule books and enforcement approach for hackney carriage and private hire licensing in Gloucester.

Purpose of licensing

Gloucester City Council licenses hackney carriage and private hire vehicles, drivers and operators to:

  • Protect public health and safety
  • Establish a professional and respected trade
  • Ensure access to efficient and effective local transport
  • Protect the environment
  • Improve standards and visibility in support of city regeneration

Legislation

The licensing scheme is governed by the following legislation:

  • Town Police Clauses Act 1847
  • Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1976 (Part II)
  • Equality Act 2010 – assistance dogs and non-discrimination
  • Licensing Act 2003 – restrictions on selling alcohol in vehicles
  • Police and Criminal Evidence Act 1984 (PACE) – interviews under caution
  • Department for Transport Statutory Taxi and Private Hire Vehicle Standards (July 2020)

Council strategy

The Corporate Plan 2025 to 2028 sets out the following priorities for taxi and private hire licensing:

  • Work with operators to increase the number of zero-emission hackney carriage and private hire vehicles in Gloucester
  • Retain Purple Flag status for the night-time economy
  • Improve safety in public spaces, with a particular focus on women and girls

Operational guidance

The following operational standards apply:

Enforcement

The council has a written enforcement and complaints policy that includes a penalty points scheme and procedures for immediate suspension or revocation where public safety requires it.

Vehicle testing

All vehicles must be tested at an approved testing station and hold a Licensing Authority Vehicle Test Certificate.

National register

The council uses the National Register of Taxi Licence Refusals and Revocations (NR3) to check all new and renewal applications and to record its own refusals and revocations.

Record keeping

Operators must keep booking records with mandatory fields for at least 6 months. Records must be produced to council officers or police on request.

Time-based requirements

Driver self-reporting
Within 48 hours of a relevant event
Accident notification
Within 72 hours
Renewal submissions
Before expiry of current licence

Quality standards

  • Enhanced DBS checks for drivers every 6 months via the DBS Update Service
  • Vehicle inspections annually, and every 6 months for vehicles aged 8 or more years
  • Operator booking records must include all mandatory fields and be kept for at least 6 months

Consistency and accountability

Shared standards across Gloucestershire

Gloucester City Council has adopted common standards with other Gloucestershire licensing authorities. These include joint authorisation of enforcement officers and information-sharing agreements to improve consistency across the county.

Delegated decisions

Licensing decisions are made under a table of delegations. Licensing officers, the Licensing and Enforcement Sub-Committee, and Full Committee each have defined responsibilities.

Right of appeal

How to appeal a decision

If you disagree with a licensing decision, you have 21 days to appeal to the Magistrates' Court (or Crown Court where applicable). The right of appeal does not apply to immediate vehicle suspension under section 68 of the Local Government (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act 1976.
