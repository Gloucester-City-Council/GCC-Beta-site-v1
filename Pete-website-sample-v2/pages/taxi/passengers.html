Using taxis and private hire in Gloucester

Your rights as a passenger when using a licensed hackney carriage or private hire vehicle. Find out how to complain or report lost property.

Fares and payment

Hackney carriages (taxis) within Gloucester

The driver must not charge you more than the amount shown on the taximeter. The meter and a tariff card must be clearly visible to you throughout the journey.

Journeys outside Gloucester or private hire

The fare or method of calculation must be agreed with you before the journey begins.

Card payment

All licensed vehicles accept contactless or card payment. You can ask for a receipt at any time.

Assistance dogs

Licensed drivers must carry guide dogs, hearing dogs and other assistance dogs. There is no extra charge for your dog. The driver cannot refuse unless they hold a medical exemption certificate from the council.

Smokefree vehicles

All licensed hackney carriage and private hire vehicles are smokefree at all times. This includes cigarettes, e-cigarettes and vaping. Every vehicle must display a 'No Smoking' sign.

Seatbelts

All passengers must wear a seatbelt. Drivers are responsible for ensuring passengers under the age of 14 wear a seatbelt.

Wheelchair access

All newly licensed hackney carriage vehicles in Gloucester are wheelchair accessible. Wheelchair-accessible vehicles must have:

  • A ramp that can be locked down and stored
  • Anchorage points for both the wheelchair and the occupant
  • Grab handles for passengers
  • A doorway of at least 75 cm wide and 1.2 m high
  • A passenger compartment of at least 1.3 m high
  • Non-slip treads on steps and ramps

Vehicle safety checks

Every licensed vehicle is inspected annually. Vehicles aged 8 years or more are inspected every 6 months. All vehicles must carry:

  • A fire extinguisher (BSEN3, 1 kg dry powder, in date)
  • A first aid kit that meets Health and Safety Executive minimum standards

Some vehicles may have CCTV cameras or dashcams approved by the council. These must comply with data protection law.

How to make a complaint

Every licensed vehicle displays information about how to complain, including the council's contact details, the vehicle plate number and the website address.

The council records and investigates all complaints. Serious cases may be referred to the police.

Make a complaint

Email: licensing@gloucester.gov.uk

When you contact us, it helps to have the vehicle plate number and the date and time of your journey.

Lost property

Drivers must check the vehicle for lost property after every journey. If property is not claimed within 48 hours, the driver must hand it to the council or the police and get a receipt.
