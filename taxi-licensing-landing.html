What you need to become a driver

To apply for a Hackney Carriage or Private Hire driver licence, you must meet all these requirements.

⏱️ Plan ahead – this takes time

You'll need criminal checks (DBS), medical examination, and to pass knowledge tests. Allow 2–3 months before your intended start date. Check processing times with us before applying.

Before you apply, you must have:

  • Valid UK or EU driving licence (minimum 2 years held)
  • Enhanced DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service) check
  • Medical examination to Group 2 DVLA standards
  • Pass knowledge test (Hackney Carriage) or literacy/numeracy test (Private Hire)
  • Proof of right to work in the UK
  • Suitable references (character)

What vehicles can be licensed

Your vehicle must meet strict standards to be licensed as a Hackney Carriage or Private Hire vehicle.

All vehicles must:

  • Not exceed 7 years from first registration
  • Have minimum 4 doors
  • Seat maximum 8 passengers
  • Meet Euro 6 emission standards
  • Have valid MOT certificate
  • Have insurance covering "hire and reward"
  • Pass vehicle inspection by approved testing station

If it's a Hackney Carriage (white taxi), it must also:

  • Be white
  • Display Gloucester City taxi roof sign
  • Display blue licence plates (front and rear)
  • Have approved taximeter installed and tested
  • Display current tariff card inside
  • Carry first aid kit and fire extinguisher

If it's a Private Hire vehicle, it must:

  • NOT be white
  • Display yellow licence plates (front and rear)
  • Display yellow bus lane sticker
  • Display "No insurance if not pre-booked" stickers

Run a Private Hire operation

If you want to dispatch Private Hire vehicles for passenger work, you need an operator licence.

What's a Private Hire operator?

A licensed operator is a business that takes bookings and dispatches licensed vehicles and drivers. You control who drives your vehicles and ensure they meet all licensing requirements.

As an operator, you must:

  • Maintain a booking system and record of all journeys
  • Only use licensed drivers and vehicles
  • Ensure all drivers have valid licences and DBS checks
  • Provide comprehensive insurance for your operation
  • Meet vehicle maintenance and safety standards
  • Keep records of complaints and handle them properly
  • Notify the council of any changes to your business
