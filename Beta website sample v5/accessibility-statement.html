Accessibility statement

This accessibility statement applies to the Gloucester City Council website at gloucestercity.gov.uk.

This website is run by Gloucester City Council. We want as many people as possible to be able to use this website. For example, that means you should be able to:

  • zoom in up to 400% without the text spilling off the screen
  • navigate most of the website using just a keyboard
  • navigate most of the website using speech recognition software
  • listen to most of the website using a screen reader (including the most recent versions of JAWS, NVDA and VoiceOver)
  • change colours, contrast levels and fonts using browser or device settings

We have also made the website text as simple as possible to understand, aiming for a maximum reading age of 11.

AbilityNet has advice on making your device easier to use if you have a disability.

How accessible this website is

We know some parts of this website are not fully accessible. The known issues are listed in the 'non-accessible content' section below.

Feedback and contact information

If you need information on this website in a different format like accessible PDF, large print, easy read, audio recording or braille:

  • email: accessibility@gloucestercity.gov.uk
  • phone: 01452 396 396

We will consider your request and get back to you in 5 working days.

Reporting accessibility problems with this website

We are always looking to improve the accessibility of this website. If you find any problems not listed on this page or think we are not meeting accessibility requirements, contact:

  • email: accessibility@gloucestercity.gov.uk
  • phone: 01452 396 396

Enforcement procedure

The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (the 'accessibility regulations'). If you're not happy with how we respond to your complaint, contact the Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS) .

Technical information about this website's accessibility

Gloucester City Council is committed to making its website accessible, in accordance with the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018.

Compliance status

This website is partially compliant with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) version 2.2 AA standard, due to the non-compliances listed below.

Non-accessible content

The content listed below is non-accessible for the following reasons.

Non-compliance with the accessibility regulations

  • Some older PDF documents do not meet accessibility standards. We are working to fix these or provide accessible HTML alternatives by March 2027. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criterion 4.1.2 (name, role, value).
  • Some images in older news articles do not have adequate alternative text. We are reviewing and updating these by June 2026. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criterion 1.1.1 (non-text content).
  • Some third-party embedded content (such as mapping tools) may not be fully keyboard navigable. We are working with our suppliers to improve this. This fails WCAG 2.2 success criterion 2.1.1 (keyboard).

What we are doing to improve accessibility

We review our website's accessibility at least every 12 months and after significant changes. Our accessibility improvement plan includes:

  • replacing all inaccessible PDFs with accessible HTML pages by March 2027
  • auditing all images for alternative text by June 2026
  • working with third-party suppliers to improve accessibility of embedded content
  • training all content editors in writing accessible content
  • carrying out user testing with people who use assistive technology

Preparation of this accessibility statement

This statement was prepared on 20 February 2026. It was last reviewed on 20 February 2026.

This website was last tested in February 2026. The test was carried out by the Gloucester City Council digital team.

We tested a sample of pages including the homepage, service landing pages and online forms. We used automated tools (Axe, Wave) and manual testing with keyboard navigation and screen readers (JAWS, NVDA and VoiceOver).

We will review and update this statement regularly, at minimum every 12 months.
